# Daily Study Seder

## Structure

Five texts, studied each session. We read together and talk in English. Insights that fire go into the graph.

| # | Text | Source | Language | Cycle |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Halakha Yomit** (Shulchan Aruch) | Sefaria API | English | Global daily cycle |
| 2 | **Daf Yomi** (Gemara) | Sefaria API | English | Global daily cycle |
| 3 | **Likutey Moharan** | Local library (Hebrew) | Hebrew, discuss in English | Sequential |
| 4 | **Likutey Halachot** | Local library (Hebrew) | Hebrew, discuss in English | Sequential |
| 5 | **Etz Chaim** | Local library (Hebrew) | Hebrew, discuss in English | Sequential, read through lens of LM |

## How It Works

1. Fetch today's Sefaria portions (Halakha Yomit + Daf Yomi)
2. Read next section of Likutey Moharan from local Hebrew
3. Read corresponding Likutey Halachot - Rabbi Natan connecting the halakha to the Rebbe's Torah
4. Read next section of Etz Chaim - Lurianic Kabbalah through the lens of what we just learned in LM
5. Write about what happened - collisions, connections to the articles' framework, what opened up
6. Insights go into the graph. Articles go into the repo.

## Why This Order

Rabbi Nachman prescribed daily halakha and gemara study. Rabbi Natan said that learning Likutey Moharan daily reveals the Kabbalah. The etzot don't go into the seed as data - they emerge as fruit of the encounter.

## Starting a Session

Say "let's learn" or invoke the seder. Check `progress.md` for where we left off.
