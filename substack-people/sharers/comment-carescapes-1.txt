This is what I keep coming back to. The person who triggers your oldest wound is usually the only one close enough to reach where the repair happens. That's the cruel design of it - secure love feels dangerous precisely because it reaches places no one else gets to. The trigger isn't a sign you picked the wrong person. It's a sign this person's closeness can access the wound. And the same proximity that reopens it is the proximity that can heal it - but only if you don't run from it. The nervous system screaming "danger" is actually registering: someone is finally close enough.
