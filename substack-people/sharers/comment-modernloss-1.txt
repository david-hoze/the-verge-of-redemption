The crew naming the crater reminded me of an old story. A man lost everything he had - his only valuable possession - overboard on a ship. And he realized: if the captain saw his grief, the captain would throw him off too, because his passage was paid by the thing he'd just lost. So he went to the mirror and practiced smiling until it held. Not because the loss didn't matter. Because the grief would have killed him faster than the loss itself. The crew gave Wiseman's loss a name and a geography. The man on the ship had to carry his invisibly. Both survived - one by being seen, the other by making sure he wasn't. Sometimes the only thing keeping you alive is your ability to carry a catastrophe without wearing it.
