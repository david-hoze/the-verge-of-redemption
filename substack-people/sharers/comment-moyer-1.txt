Something that reframed this for me: yelling doesn't communicate authority. It communicates the absence of authority. When you erupt, your child learns two things at once - that nobody listens until someone screams, and that the adult in the room just lost control. The child who "won" by making the parent lose composure didn't win anything. They lost the one thing they actually needed: a parent who is calm enough to lean on. Staying calm isn't passivity. It's the only thing that keeps you in charge.
