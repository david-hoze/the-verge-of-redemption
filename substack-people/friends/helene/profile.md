## Her Framework

### Integration Model (Intimacy Notes I)
Integration = arriving at the center of observation where perception stops being input. Green-or-nothing signal: no spectrum, no yellow. What's real passes through. What isn't doesn't. "When only what is real is allowed, very little qualifies." The cost of clarity is that most things don't survive it. Aloneness (clarity without company) differs from loneliness (vulnerability as identity).

### Seven Phases of Structural Integration
A full neurocognitive model of how integrated nervous systems develop. Not mysticism - grounded in clinical experience and neurobiology. Key insight: "Clarity arrives before tolerance." Early phases involve painful rawness as dampening mechanisms weaken. The world becomes unbearably loud. Many mistake this for regression.

Phases: (1) old structure failing, (2) dismantling against resistance, (3) recognition without stability, (4) hermitage/calibration under reduced distortion, (5) early re-entry with structural boundaries, (6) stabilization with selective permeability, (7) mature integration where identity matters less than function.

### Recognition vs. Projection (When Nothing Starts)
Projection = internal priors overriding incoming signal. Self-reinforcing, cycles without reorganization. Recognition = incoming signal that exceeds prior influence, forces system restructuring. Her sequence: body first, then recognition, then attraction - never the reverse. Recognition "remains invariant until reactivated by direct signal." The paradox: genuine recognition can exist without matching external outcome.
