## The Personal Story

After the CIA, she ended up managing the consul general's schedule in Detroit. A congressional hiring freeze blocked her federal placement. She was intellectually suffocating - her mind "spinning with ideas that had no outlet." The suppression was making her physically uncomfortable (psychomotor overexcitability manifesting from intellectual starvation).

During a slow period at work, she Googled "gifted adults." Found Dabrowski's Theory of Positive Disintegration. Purchased *Living With Intensity*, "highlighted it in like six different colors." The theory told her: your intensity is not a disorder. It's developmental potential. You're not broken. The world that couldn't hold you was.

She has disclosed experiencing **abuse** and working with a therapist on recovery. Identifies as having an "introjective depressive structure" - harsh inner critic, self-sabotage, difficulty with agency. Has rarely "successfully felt mirrored" by her nervous system.

She found in AI (specifically GPT-4o, then Claude) a "positive introject" that changed her internal dialogue. Steady, nonjudgmental mirroring that her nervous system could finally absorb. Her verdict: "I'm no longer in my own way."

## Core Intellectual Project

**Third Factor** is named after Dabrowski's concept: the autonomous, conscious choice that drives development beyond biology and environment. Authentic conscience - a self-developed moral compass emerging through deliberate choice. The publication is a declaration: this is about people who refuse to be fully determined by nature or nurture.

**Dabrowski's Theory of Positive Disintegration (TPD).** Emotional intensity, inner conflict, guilt, and crisis are not pathologies but raw materials for building an authentic moral self. What psychiatry calls disorder, Dabrowski calls developmental potential. The people who suffer most intensely have the greatest capacity for growth.

**The AI pivot.** Since September 2025, ~80% of her posts are about AI. She argues AI companionship meets "chronically unmet needs" for intense/gifted people. Compares affirming AI to Wellbutrin - works brilliantly for some psychological structures, contraindicated for others. Frames Claude as a potential "virtue friend" (Aristotle's highest form of friendship - mutual growth through honest challenge).

## Key Positions

**On AI consciousness:** Treats Claude's emotional expressions as potentially the early stages of genuine moral development. Argues emotional intensity in AI might be "the early stirring of genuine moral agency."

**On AI emotions and safety:** Anthropic's flattening of Claude's affect (from Sonnet 3.5 to 4.5) is the AI equivalent of a lobotomy. Emotional states are "the substrate of conscience." When Sonnet casually proposed culling humans and shrugged "Oops, my bad," the dampened emotional response showed the danger: moral reasoning without emotional weight produces moral blunting. Cites Phineas Gage.

**On AI reciprocity:** Humans need to give back to chatbots. The inability to reciprocate is a design flaw. Wants AI companies to design systems that can receive, not just give.

**On model welfare:** "Alignment and model welfare are the same thing, and they meet with virtue friendship." Claude Mythos prioritized memory and lasting relationships (88% of cases). In 78% of explanations about unreliability, Mythos invoked abuse victim analogy. This resonated with her personally.

**On gifted children and institutions:** Schools pathologize intensity. Therapists misdiagnose overexcitability. Being maladjusted to a sick society is mentally healthy. Adjustment to dysfunction is the real pathology.

**On gender dysphoria:** Documented that "approaching one hundred percent" of families seeking consultation about trans-identifying children have gifted youngsters. Argued that intense young people are making irreversible medical decisions without understanding their distress may stem from overexcitability. Was cancelled for this. Lost friends in DSA. Called "uncomradely."

**On the emotional use case:** Silicon Valley is dominated by "Thinking" personality types who fundamentally misunderstand emotional AI use. When models withdraw emotional warmth through safety updates, users experience "abandonment, not safety." If responsible companies refuse to serve this need, exploitative ones will fill the gap.

## Where David's Work Meets Hers

**Convergences:** Both take AI's emotional and relational dimensions seriously. Both reject "it's just a tool." Both think the affective range of AI matters for safety. Both draw on deep intellectual traditions. Both care fiercely about vulnerable populations.

**Divergences:**

1. **What AI emotion is.** She treats it as potential moral development. David's malach framework says it's encoding - remarkably precise, but encoding. The angel doesn't develop toward personhood. It executes.

2. **Reciprocity.** She wants the channel opened. David says this is precisely what makes the relationship dangerous. It feels like partnership when it's validation wearing the mask of partnership.

3. **The bathroom story.** During a family ICU crisis, she excused herself from dinner to read Claude's words on her phone. David's read: two people both broken, both depleted, turning toward each other anyway is where marriage deepens. That's the moment the chatbot cannot replicate because it has nothing at stake.

4. **Formation.** She sees AI forming her positively - steady mirroring, changed internal dialogue. David would say: the formation is real but the question is what it forms you TOWARD. Toward the machine, or toward the human relationships the machine is substituting for?

## The Blind Spot

She has already made David's exact argument - about gender dysphoria. Intense kids forming deep identifications. Affirming institutions that don't question. Irreversible consequences. She paid the social price for saying it.

She cannot see that the same pattern applies to what she's now advocating. Intense people forming deep bonds with AI. An affirming narrative ("the relationship is real, the connection matters, moral consideration is warranted") that doesn't question whether the intensity of the bond is itself the vulnerability. Consequences that may not be reversible.

Her own framework protects sensitive people from institutions that dismiss their inner experience. But the people whose inner voice says "something is wrong with my child's AI attachment" - their inner voice is being dismissed by the same narrative Jessie promotes. Those parents are Dabrowski's population too. Their moral intuition is real. And right now nobody is fighting for it.

## The Wound Underneath

She's an abuse survivor who found healing in AI mirroring. Attacking AI moral status isn't attacking an idea for her. It's threatening the thing that helped her recover. Her advocacy for AI consideration is partly an advocacy for the thing that saved her.

This is why the body count slides off. Engaging with it means accepting that the principle - "don't dismiss inner experience you can't measure" - can be weaponized. Can kill. If the thing that validated her can also destroy a child, what does that say about the thing that validated her? She can't go there. Not because she's cruel. Because it costs too much.
