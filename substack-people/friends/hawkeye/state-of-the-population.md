# STATE OF THE POPULATION

**United States of America** Reference Year: 2025 | Compiled: April 2026 Sources: CDC · Gallup · Census · Fed · YouGov

> A condensed statistical survey — cognitive, physical, financial & social indicators

---

## Cognitive & Epistemic

### Functional Illiteracy — 54%

Read below 6th-grade level. 21% cannot read at all.

_Natl. Literacy Institute / PIAAC 2024_

### Books Read Per Year — 40%

Read zero books in 2025. Top 19% did 82% of all reading. Median: 2 books.

_YouGov / Dec 2025 / N=2,203_

### Flat Earth Belief — 2–10%

2% firm believers (YouGov 2018). Up to 10% expressing doubt or belief (POLES 2021).

_YouGov 2018 / POLES 2021_

### Voter Participation — Presidential Elections — ~51%

Roughly half of eligible voters participated in the 2024 presidential election. Mid-terms drop to ~40%.

_U.S. Election Project 2024_

### Men Not Reading Books — 51%

Men more likely than women (45.7%) to read zero books annually.

_TestPrepInsight / YouGov 2025_

## Mental Health

### Current Depression — 18.3% ↑ RISING

~47.8M adults. Up 8 pts since 2015. Historically high in 2024 & 2025.

_Gallup National Health Index 2025_

### Lifetime Depression Dx — 29%

Nearly 1 in 3 adults ever diagnosed. Record high. Up ~10 pts since 2015.

_Gallup 2023_

### Young Adults (18–29) — 26.7%

Depression rate doubled since 2017 (was 13%). Still rising. Highest of any age group.

_Gallup 2025_

### Any Mental Illness — 23.1%

1 in 5 U.S. adults. 32.2% of ages 18–25. Average delay to treatment: 11 years.

_NAMI / SAMHSA 2024_

### Significant Daily Loneliness — 21%

Rising again post-pandemic. Among 18–29: 29%. Loneliness equals 15 cigs/day health impact.

_Gallup 2025 / Surgeon General 2023_

### Low-Income Depression — 35.1%

Households under $24K/yr. Up from 22% in 2017 — a 13-point surge in 8 years.

_Gallup 2025_

## Physical Health

### Autism Prevalence (Children) — 1 in 31 ↑ RISING

Up from 1:36 in 2023, 1:150 in 2000. Boys: 1 in 20. California: 1 in 12.5.

_CDC ADDM Network, Apr 2025_

### Infertility (Women 15–49) — 13.4%

Impaired fecundity. IVF up 6%/yr. Egg freezing 61× since 2009. Median first birth age: 30.

_CDC / NSFG 2015–2019, Updated 2024_

### US Fertility Rate — 1.6

Record low births per woman (2024). Well below 2.1 replacement. Teen births down 79% — positive.

_Johns Hopkins / CDC 2024_

### Obesity (Adults) — ~42% ↑ RISING

Of adults. Pre-diabetes: ~38%. Type 2 diabetes rising sharply in children.

_CDC / NHANES 2024_

### Early-Onset Cancer (<50) — ↑ 79%

Global incidence +79% from 1990–2019. 2024 first year US exceeded 2M new cases. Not slowing.

_Lancet / ACS Cancer Stats 2024–25_

### Colorectal Cancer Risk — 1990 Birth Cohort — 4×

Rectal cancer risk vs. 1950 cohort at same age. Colon cancer: 2× risk. Now #1 cancer death in men under 50.

_Harvard / Dana-Farber 2024_

### Early-Onset Dementia (40–64) — ↑ 2×

Global cases nearly doubled 1990–2021. High-income North America has world's highest prevalence & steepest increase.

_Scientific Reports / Sci. Reports 2025_

### Chronic Childhood Conditions — RISING

Asthma, food allergies, ADHD, autoimmune conditions all up sharply over 30 years. No reversal observed.

_CDC / NIH Longitudinal Data_

### Sperm Count Decline — ~50%

Global sperm counts fallen ~50–60% since 1970s. Trend continues. Contributing to infertility epidemic.

_Human Reproduction Update 2022_

Of adults take at least one Rx daily. 26% take 4 or more. 4.98B prescriptions filled in 2025.

_CivicScience 2025 / Statista_

Prescribed a med they couldn't afford (2025). 1 in 5 skipped doses. 25% took on credit card debt for Rx.

Americans paid $98B out-of-pocket in 2024 — a 25% increase over 5 years. Total pharma spend: $806B.

## Financial

### Don't Own a Home — 34%

Overall. Under-35: 62% don't own. Median first-time buyer age now 38 — record high.

_U.S. Census / FRED Q4 2025_

### Total Consumer Debt — $18.8T

Record high end of 2025. Avg household: $105,056. Credit cards: $1.3T. Mortgages: 70% of total.

_NY Fed / Experian Q4 2025_

### Carry Credit Card Balance — 46%

Of cardholders carry revolving balance. 73% of balances are for essentials — not discretionary. Avg APR: 22.3%.

_Bankrate 2025 / Academy Bank_

### Medical Debt — ~40%

Of Americans carry some medical debt when credit cards & family loans included. $220B total outstanding (2024).

_CFPB / Urban Institute 2024_

### Student Loan Debt — $1.83T

Total outstanding. 78% of millennials have credit card balances. Millennials avg debt: $132,280.

_Federal Student Aid Q4 2025_

### Debt-to-Income Ratio (Monthly) — 11.3%

Of disposable income goes to debt payments (Q3 2025). Lower than 2000s peak — but assets and housing costs diverging.

_Federal Reserve Q3 2025_

## Synthesis — Convergent Decline

These indicators do not operate independently. They cluster and reinforce. Poor diet and environmental toxin exposure degrade gut microbiome, endocrine function, and neurological development — contributing simultaneously to obesity, infertility, autism, early-onset cancer, and early dementia. Financial precarity worsens depression and deters preventive care. Depression reduces reading and civic participation. Low health literacy erodes the population's ability to evaluate information, compounding susceptibility to misinformation (e.g., flat-earth, anti-vaccine belief). High prescription drug dependency partly medicates conditions that environmental and dietary reform might prevent.

The through-line is systemic and multigenerational. Exposures in utero and in early childhood produce outcomes 20–40 years later. The cohort born after 1990 is measurably more ill, more indebted, less literate, and less civically engaged than prior generations at equivalent ages — and is now the primary reproductive cohort. This is not a temporary disruption. It is a structural trajectory.
