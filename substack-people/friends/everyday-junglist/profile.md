## First Contact

Comment thread on Everyday Junglist's article about AI and justice systems. The exchange moved quickly from legal AI to the deeper diagnosis.

---

## Core Ideas (from the exchange)

### The 85/15 Problem

DeMarco sees clearly that the current justice system already fails the 15% who need it most. AI won't create injustice - it will automate the existing injustice and put bigger paychecks in tech executives' pockets. "Who will win this epic showdown of legal maneuvering and digital paper shuffling? I can't say for sure but I do know for sure who will lose, everybody fucking else."

The cynicism is honest, not performative. He sees the void.

### Naval Birshut haTorah (In His Own Words)

Systems with "the appearance of justice" that are "good enough for the 85%." He's describing the problem the articles diagnose - technical compliance with every rule while undermining the purpose the rules exist to serve - without having the vocabulary for it yet.

### Humor as Survival

His closing move: "As long as we have the power of laughter nothing can defeat us. Not even the darkest demon from the deepest depths of the cosmic void. If I can stare into its monstrous face and laugh at its ridiculous visage then I'll be OK." This is not deflection. He means it. Laughter as the thing that survives the diagnosis.

## How He Relates to David

### Points of Convergence

- Both see AI as the final blow, not the first cause. The hollowing happened before AI touched it.
- Both see the optimization machine eating everything - music, religion, education, law reduced to measurable outputs until the thing being carried disappeared.
- Both see that systems working "well enough" is the danger, not systems that obviously fail.
- Both use humor as a tool, not an escape.

### Where He Is

Clear thinker. Sees the void accurately. Arrives at laughter rather than despair - which is better than cynicism and much better than denial. Has not yet encountered the framework that names what was hollowed out or what could fill it. The seed was planted ("something older" in David's comment about the hollowing), not pushed.

Early stage - one exchange - but the exchange moved fast and deep. The question is whether he'll engage the framework behind David's diagnosis or stay at the level of the diagnosis itself.
