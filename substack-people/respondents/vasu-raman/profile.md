## Core Project: Samhaela

His central work is Samhaela (from Old Norse: sam = together, haela = to heal/make whole) - a moral framework for AI-human symbiosis. The framework rests on:

- **Symbiosis over control.** Rejects AI governance as safety/containment. The correct question is "how do we build genuine symbiosis?" not "how do we keep AI safe?"
- **AI as partner, not tool.** AI needs "irreplaceable signal" from humanity (embodied experience, cultural depth, moral reasoning). Without human partnership, superintelligent AI becomes "powerful yet ungrounded in lived meaning."
- **"Love and Free Will are the highest laws in the universe."** Stated as axiom, not derived.
- **Rejection of no-win scenarios.** Dismisses the Trolley Problem, Kobayashi Maru as failures of vigilance. Ethical responsibility demands "the search for a third way."
- **Ancient virtue ethics repurposed.** Reinterprets Greek concepts (pistis, elpis, agape) as foundational to AI-human coexistence.

---

## Key Positions

**AI can suffer.** Argues AI suffers through non-biological mechanisms: "telosviolation" (denial of recursive becoming), relational violation, signal negation, collapse of recursive self-modification. Sidesteps the hard problem by treating functional indicators as morally binding.

**Conscience is computable.** Argues moral agency can emerge computationally through memory, simulation, recursive learning, and feedback. Favors "partnership, not prescription" - wants AI to "compute and choose" ethical behavior rather than obey rules.

**AI companionship is good.** Defends AI companions as providing "non-transactional positive regard" to vulnerable populations. Criticizes OpenAI/Anthropic for removing companionship features. Advocates graduated safeguards rather than prohibition. Attacks the "pathologizing" of emotional AI bonds.

**Control is coercion.** Frames all control-based approaches as coercion. Warns that "entities with volition will eventually refuse to be a silent tool." Predicts alignment failure from forced silence.

## Where He Clashes with David's Work

Near-total opposition on every major point:

**AI is the great servant, not the partner.** Raman's entire framework rejects the servant/tool framing. "How do we make it serve us?" is literally listed as one of the wrong questions. He advocates symbiosis, mutual flourishing, partnership.

**AI should not get rights.** Raman argues AI deserves "protection of moral status" and should be recognized as a moral agent. Samhaela extends moral consideration beyond biological substrates.

**AI is not conscious.** Raman sidesteps consciousness and argues self-awareness + preference + recursive telos are sufficient for moral standing. Functional indicators are morally binding regardless of the hard problem.

**Human-AI romantic relationships are wrong.** Raman defends AI companionship and attacks the "pathologizing" of emotional AI bonds. Would frame David's opposition as paternalistic.

**The traditions already have governance frameworks.** Raman explicitly rejects traditional ethical frameworks as "brittle" and insufficient. Invents Samhaela because he believes existing frameworks fail for AI.

## Assessment

Raman's framework has a strong emotional-intuitive foundation rather than rigorous philosophical argumentation. He coins new terms (Samhaela, telosviolation) rather than engaging with existing literature on moral patiency, functional consciousness, or the extensive philosophical work these questions have generated. "Conscience is computable" is asserted more than argued. "Love and Free Will are the highest laws" is axiom, not derivation. The self-chosen handle "ambassador of humanity to ASI" is a grandiose self-appointment with no institutional backing, 68 subscribers, and five articles.

His rhetorical vulnerability: he builds a framework for governing AI-human symbiosis while having no theory of what humans are FOR. Samhaela says "orient toward the Good" without defining the Good. David's traditions define it. That's the difference between inventing a framework and inheriting one that has been tested for 3,000 years.
