# Comments on Hannah Spier's Articles - Draft

## 1. "The Spectrum": How Autism Was Hijacked By Narcissists

The three populations you identified - the eccentric introvert, the undisciplined child, the Cluster B adult - is the clearest map I've seen of how diagnostic expansion works. Each has a real need the autism label obscures. The eccentric child needs to be left alone. The undisciplined child needs boundaries. The Cluster B adult needs accountability, not a neurotype that validates every impulse. The nonverbal child who needs lifelong care disappears into the same spectrum as the TikTok "late diagnosis." You can't serve both with the same category.

## 2. ADHD: The Result of a Gaping Diagnostic Hole

The sharpest piece in your body of work. Psychiatry recognizes extreme conscientiousness as OCPD, extreme neuroticism as BPD, extreme agreeableness as dependent PD - but the moment openness goes extreme and conscientiousness bottoms out, it invents a neurodevelopmental disorder. The PID-5 items next to the ADHD symptom list aren't parallel constructs. They're identical constructs through different moral lenses. That no study has directly compared this personality configuration against diagnosed ADHD patients tells you everything about the field's commitments.

---

## 3. The Trauma Myth of Borderline Personality Disorder

The circularity in Linehan's model - "I have BPD because my parents were invalidating, and I know they were invalidating because I have BPD" - is a genuine methodological problem most clinical training breezes past. The prospective studies point matters most: follow children forward instead of asking adults to remember backward, and early trauma predicts psychopathology in general but does not specifically produce BPD. The clinical world has been running on "trauma causes borderline" without the evidence.

---

## 4. How the Borderline Personality Develops

The reinforcement model is more honest than the trauma model. The "attractiveness subsidy" names something real - the halo effect doesn't just shape first impressions, it shapes developmental trajectories by altering which behaviors get corrected. Fewer consequences means less-shaped personality.

One thing I notice across the full BPD series: the analysis locates the strategy but moves past the suffering quickly. I think they're structurally inseparable. The neuroticism is genuinely painful AND the behaviors are genuinely predatory. Holding both is what makes the analysis medicine rather than a verdict.

---

## 5. The False Promises of Dialectical Behavioural Therapy

Containment vs. cure is the right frame. The deeper point - that framing behavior as a "skills deficit" rather than a strategy removes moral agency - applies far beyond DBT. It's the structural error of the entire therapeutic framework.

Your work raises a question across the full arc: if medicalization is the wrong frame and prosecution is the wrong frame, what's the third option? I think it starts with the thing your diagnostic pieces do that the BPD pieces don't quite reach - seeing the person inside the pattern before naming the pattern.
