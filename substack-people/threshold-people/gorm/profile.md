# Gorm

## Platform
- Substack: Metaphorician (https://metaphorician.substack.com/)

## Who
Philosopher. Author of "Virtualism" - a framework treating all worldviews as virtual perspectives, none permitted a final say. Draws on Santayana ("animal faith"), Kant (regulative ideas), and skeptical tradition. Careful thinker - when challenged on Nachman, he went and researched the source material rather than dismissing it.

## Position
Epistemic gap must stay empty to function. Two categories: empty gap (honest skepticism) or furnished gap (theology/closure). Believes in "reality" as a regulative idea but resists ontological commitment. The gap prevents error; furnishing it brings closure.

## What makes him threshold
He's doing the work. He researched Nachman. He engages with the actual argument, not a straw man. His strongest move - "the more you furnish the gap, the closer you get to closure" - is a real challenge that required a real answer. He admitted the Talmudic practical-decision point might find parallels in his future work. The door is open.

## Vulnerability
His framework has a structural blind spot: his own "reality as regulative idea" is a commitment to fullness beyond the epistemic frame - the same move he calls theology when someone else makes it. He hasn't noticed this yet.
