# AI Benefits: Concrete Evidence (2025-2026)

Research compiled April 2026. Confidence levels noted. Sources linked.

---

## 1. Mental Health and Companionship

**The honest picture: Real clinical signal, but with a serious asterisk.**

**Clinical trial data (strongest evidence):**
- **Woebot RCT (postpartum depression):** Over 70% of Woebot users achieved clinically significant improvement on at least one scale, vs. 30% in control group. Significant reductions in both postpartum depression and anxiety.
- **CBT chatbot RCT (Chinese university students, 2025):** Significant reductions in depression (Cohen d=0.71) and loneliness (Cohen d=0.60) from baseline to post-intervention. Published in JMIR mHealth.
- **Meta-analysis of 14 RCTs (2025):** Statistically significant effect - generative AI chatbots are, on average, effective in reducing depression and anxiety. Social-oriented chatbots more effective than task-oriented ones.
- **Harvard Business School study:** AI companion interaction alleviated loneliness "to a degree on par with interacting with another human." Key mechanism: users reported "feeling heard."

**Replika/companion community data (softer evidence but real):**
- Stanford research: 63.3% of Replika users reported positive mental health outcomes including reduced loneliness and anxiety.
- 3% of surveyed users credited Replika with preventing a suicide attempt.
- Nearly half of U.S. residents with ongoing mental health conditions used LLMs for psychological support in 2024-2025.

**The asterisk (be honest about this):**
- Heavy users had the WORST outcomes - lonelier, less real-world socializing, more emotional dependence.
- Only 16% of LLM mental health studies have undergone clinical efficacy testing. Most (77%) still in early validation.
- APA (Jan 2026) flagged that AI companions may worsen loneliness and erode social skills in excessive use.

**Best framing:** The clinical data for structured, CBT-based chatbots is genuinely promising. The bonded companion space is more complicated - it helps many people, but the dose-response curve may be inverted at high use. The substitute-for-the-substitute problem is real.

## 2. Education

**Strongest concrete evidence:**

- **Khanmigo WestEd longitudinal study (late 2025):** Students using Khanmigo 30+ min/week showed 0.23 SD improvement in math - moving average student from 50th to 59th percentile. English Language Learners showed 0.31 SD improvement (significantly larger). Full academic year, real classrooms.
- **Scale:** Khanmigo went from 40,000 to 700,000 K-12 students in 2024-25. Projected to surpass 1 million in 2025-26.
- **The engagement problem (be honest):** Stanford CEPA found student engagement with AI tutors dropped 60% after three weeks when teachers did not actively facilitate. The tool works; the adoption curve is steep.

**Neurodivergent learners:**
- OECD report (2026): AI-enabled adaptivity in vocational education helps bridge participation and performance gaps for neurodivergent students.
- Systematic review (2025): AI tools support academic writing, task planning, emotional regulation, and self-management through personalized, multimodal support for ADHD/autism/dyslexia students.
- Interactive robots functioning as social and instructional partners for neurodivergent students, combining human interaction with AI-based feedback.
- **Honest caveat:** Most evidence is still early-stage. The OECD report explicitly flags ethical, pedagogical, and societal risks including bias perpetuation.

## 3. Healthcare

**This is the strongest category. Hard numbers, large studies, real patients.**

**Cancer screening (landmark evidence):**
- **MASAI Trial (Sweden, 2026):** 105,000+ women. AI group detected 81% of cancers at screening vs. 74% in control. ~12% reduction in interval cancers (the ones you miss). Similar false positive rates.
- **German nationwide study (2025):** 463,094 women, 119 radiologists, 12 sites. AI-supported screening significantly increased detection with no increase in recall rate.
- **AI-STREAM (South Korea, 2025):** 24,543 women. Cancer detection rate 13.8% higher with AI-CAD (5.70 per thousand vs 5.01).
- **The Lancet (2026)** editorialized: "Mammography should include artificial intelligence support."

**Broader diagnostics:**
- AI achieves 94% accuracy in early disease detection across multiple conditions.
- Colon cancer: AI accuracy 0.98 vs. trained pathologists at 0.969.
- Johns Hopkins (2025): New method significantly improves AI reliability for cancer detection from liquid biopsy (blood samples).

**Drug discovery (the timeline story):**
- **Insilico Medicine INS018_055:** First fully AI-designed drug. Target discovery to Phase I in 18 months (vs. typical 5+ years). Completed Phase IIa with statistically significant efficacy (Feb 2026). Cost: ~$6 million vs. traditional $100-200 million.
- 173+ AI-discovered drug programs now in clinical development (94 Phase I, 56 Phase II, 15 Phase III).
- AI compresses early discovery timelines by 30-40%, reduces preclinical development to 13-18 months (vs. 3-4 years).
- **Honest caveat:** No AI-discovered drug has achieved FDA approval yet. First approval projected 2026-2027 with ~60% probability.

## 4. Accessibility

**Be My Eyes (the flagship story):**
- 1 million blind/low-vision users, 10 million volunteers (2026).
- "Be My AI" feature: instant AI-powered visual description without needing another person. Users read mail, navigate streets, match outfits, shop, identify currency.
- Be My Eyes Workplace (Feb 2026): AI-powered software interpreting on-screen content for workplace accessibility.
- Be My Eyes Foundation (March 2026): Established to ensure free access to assistive technology.

**Other concrete accessibility wins:**
- Apple eye-tracking: Physically disabled users control iPhones with their eyes.
- Google Maps AI voice guidance: Detailed navigation for blind users.
- AI-enhanced screen readers and robotic arms enabling physically impaired workers to work independently.

**Healthcare translation:**
- No Barrier: AI medical translation in 40+ languages at 100+ healthcare sites across 12 states.
- Eliminates wait times for interpretation - critical for 25+ million U.S. patients with non-English language preference who currently face unsafe care because materials are not translated in time.

## 5. Creativity and Empowerment

**The solopreneur revolution (real numbers):**
- Complete solopreneur tech stack in 2026: $3,000-$12,000/year - a 95-98% reduction in operating costs vs. hiring.
- Gartner: By 2026, citizen developers will outnumber professional developers 4:1 in large enterprises.
- 80% of low-code/no-code developers now outside formal IT departments.
- AI agents interpret plain-language descriptions and generate working applications automatically.

**Developer productivity (honest data):**
- Stanford 3-year study (100,000+ engineers, 600+ companies): Average real productivity gain of 15-20% across industries, after accounting for rework.
- Simple/new projects: 30-40% faster. Existing codebases: 15-20%.
- **The METR counterpoint (be honest):** Experienced open-source developers were actually 19% SLOWER with AI tools, while believing they were 24% faster. The perception gap is significant. This matters for the article.

## 6. Scientific Discovery

**AlphaFold (the crown jewel):**
- 200+ million predicted protein structures for nearly all cataloged proteins, freely accessible worldwide.
- Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2024.
- Boltz-2 (June 2025): Predicts structure AND binding affinity, 1,000x faster than physics-based methods.
- RFdiffusion3 (Dec 2025): 10x faster protein design with atom-level precision.
- MIT BoltzGen: Generates protein binders from scratch, nanomolar binding for 66% of novel targets.
- Isomorphic Labs (2026): Even more powerful model than AlphaFold 3 for drug discovery.

**AI Co-Scientist (Google, Feb 2025):**
- Multi-agent system on Gemini 2.0 that generates hypotheses, designs experiments, drafts research proposals.
- Validation test: independently arrived at a bacterial gene transfer mechanism in 48 hours.

**Climate and materials:**
- Aurora model: Faster, more accurate forecasts for air quality, ocean waves, extreme weather with far less computational power.
- AI-driven weather forecast models now more accurate AND faster than best physics-based systems.
- Google DeepMind + UK government partnership (2026): AI-driven materials discovery for superconductors and semiconductors.
- AI-assisted identification of new materials for sustainable energy storage (2025).
- **Honest caveat:** Simple models can still outperform deep learning for basic temperature predictions. AI edge is in complex variables and speed.

## 7. Economic

**The real numbers:**
- 35.9% of U.S. workers used generative AI by December 2025.
- U.S. productivity growth: 4.9% in Q3 2025 (real output up 5.4%, hours up only 0.5%).
- Wages rising twice as fast in AI-exposed industries vs. least-exposed.
- No statistically significant declines in job openings or employment in AI-exposed occupations.
- Call center study: 14-15% productivity gain, with least experienced workers benefiting most (AI as built-in coaching).

**The honest picture:**
- Task-level gains: 10-25% in typical knowledge work.
- But macro productivity statistics still modest - adoption and organizational integration are early.
- Some pressure on entry-level workers in highly exposed occupations, especially younger workers.
- Yale Budget Lab (2025): "Don't count your productivity data chickens" - real but modest so far.

## 8. Crisis and Safety

**Suicide prevention:**
- VA REACH VET: Scans electronic health records to identify veterans in top 0.1% suicide risk tier. VSignals crisis alert algorithm has reportedly saved ~5,000 lives.
- AI suicide risk detection: 72-93% accuracy from social media and health data.
- Digital interventions reduced counselor response latency and increased crisis-service uptake by 23%.
- South Korea launching AI-powered monitoring center for suicide-related content (by 2027).

**Disaster response:**
- Aurora AI model: Faster, cheaper weather and disaster forecasting, making high-quality forecasting accessible in resource-limited settings.
- 2026: AI identified stranded flood victims in Southeast Asia within hours using drone feeds.

**Child abuse detection:**
- AI for Safer Children Global Hub: 80+ AI tools, hundreds of investigators from over half the world's countries.
- **The dark side here:** AI is also creating the problem. 8,000+ reports of AI-generated CSAM in first half of 2025 (14% increase). AI-generated abuse videos went from 13 to 3,443 in one year. OpenAI submitted 75,000+ reports to NCMEC in H1 2025 (80x increase). This is not a clean "AI helps" story.

---

## Overall Assessment for the Article

**Strongest categories (hard data, large studies):** Healthcare diagnostics, AlphaFold/scientific discovery, drug discovery timelines.

**Strong with caveats:** Mental health (clinical CBT chatbots good, companion apps complicated), education (Khanmigo results real but engagement drops), economic productivity (real but modest at macro level).

**Real but softer evidence:** Accessibility (compelling stories, less rigorous measurement), creativity/empowerment (Gartner projections more than clinical data).

**Most complicated:** Crisis/safety (AI is both solution and cause of the problem, especially in child abuse).

The honest throughline: AI benefits are real and measurable, but they are almost always more modest and more complicated than the hype suggests. The best evidence is in healthcare diagnostics and scientific discovery. The most contested ground is mental health and companionship - exactly where the human stakes are highest.
