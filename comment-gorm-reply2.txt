Gorm, the epistemology/metaphysics distinction is real and I'll accept the framing. "There exists a reality beyond experience" is metaphysical. So is "the gap is full but inaccessible." We're in the same category there.

But I want to test the hierarchy. You put epistemological withdrawal at the top - not high status, maximum abstraction. From there, all assumptions are paint on a canvas of experience.

My question is about the canvas.

When you bracket every assumption and arrive at pure subjectivity, you're not standing nowhere. You're standing inside experience itself - the one thing you can't bracket. The canvas is not an assumption. It's what remains when all assumptions are stripped away. And what remains is not empty. It's the condition for everything else.

You said pure skepticism reaches solipsism, and that solipsism isn't a good starting point for engaging in life. But why not? If the canvas were genuinely empty, solipsism would just be accurate. The fact that you need to leave it - that further assumptions are needed for life to be navigable - means the canvas itself is pushing you outward. That's not emptiness. That's generativity.

I'm not arguing your hierarchy is wrong. I'm asking: have you looked at what's holding the hierarchy up?
