# Gittin: Leaving in Order to Stay

## The Chidush

Masechet Gittin is about making leaving precise enough that staying becomes a choice.

The masechet isn't really about divorce. It's about the precision required for dissolution - so that staying is never a cage, and leaving is never a lie.

## How It Holds Across the Masechet

**Lishmah** - You must write the get for this specific woman. Not generic. Not coincidental. Two wives with the same name: a get for one cannot serve the other. You must know exactly who you're leaving.

**The dating requirement** - You must know when you're leaving. Precision in time, not just identity.

**Keritut** - "You are permitted to any man except..." is not a valid get. If you haven't fully left, you haven't left at all. Partial leaving is no leaving.

**The conditional get** - A man prepares his leaving while still present, so that if he goes, she's free. He builds the mechanism of departure in order for her to be able to stay in the world - not chained to a dead man.

**The prozbul** - Hillel left the letter of shemittah law in order to stay inside its purpose. The route-around preserved what the law was trying to do.

**The destruction** - Rabban Yohanan ben Zakkai left Jerusalem in a coffin so Torah could stay. The Zealots who refused to leave - who burned the storehouses rather than allow departure - destroyed everything. The refusal to leave destroyed the capacity to stay.

**The final Mishna** - Three opinions about grounds for divorce aren't making divorce easy. They're making the grounds honest. If you're staying, you need to know you could leave. The "could leave" is what makes the staying a choice rather than a cage.

**The altar weeps** - Because even precise leaving is loss. The Torah provides for it, the Sages made it technically sound, and when it actually happens, something real is destroyed anyway. The mechanism works and the altar mourns.

## The Line

The masechet spends nine chapters forcing precision on dissolution - not to encourage leaving, but so that staying means something. You have to leave in order to stay.
