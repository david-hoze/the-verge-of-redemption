# Simcha Requires Two Witnesses

*Seder session 1 - 13 Nisan 5786 / April 13, 2026*
*Based on the lessons of Rabbi Yaakov Moshe Salmanovich (Lehachnis Balev, Issue 3)*

## The Collision

Today's Halakha Yomit: Birkat HaTov veHaMeitiv - the bracha for goodness that is only said when another person is present and also benefits. You cannot bless the good alone.

Today's Likutey Halachot: Hashkamat HaBoker 1 - R' Natan reads "rise like a lion" through Torah 282 (Azamra). Finding the good point is how you wake from sleep.

Today's Likutey Moharan: Torah 1 - through Torah, Chet and Nun become Chen (grace), and prayers are accepted.

Today's Etz Chaim: Shaar 1 - the Tzimtzum creates the Chalal, the Kav enters it, and the light organizes into Igulim and Yosher.

What fired: The nekuda tova can't be found alone.

## The Source

R' Natan, Likutey Halachot, Hilchot Edut 4.

**Ot 9**: "צריך שניים כדי להמשיך את הדעת הזאת, שהרי צריך שניים כדי לחלק אלפים למאות" - You need two to draw down this da'at, because you need two to divide thousands into hundreds.

**Ot 11**: "לֹא יָקוּם עֵד אֶחָד בְּאִישׁ לְכָל עָוֹן וּלְכָל חַטָּאת" - One witness cannot stand against a person for any sin or transgression. R' Natan reads: one alone doesn't have the strength to stand against the Sitra Achra, which fights to keep the light above and the crumbs below, separated - "שָׁארֵי בְּחִבּוּרָא וְסַיֵּם בְּפֵרוּדָא", starts in connection, ends in separation. That's the essence of death - it separates the life from the body.

**Ot 10**: Kedusha works the opposite way - "שָׁארֵי בְּפֵרוּדָא וְסַיֵּם בְּחִבּוּרָא" - starts in separation, ends in connection. It divides alafim into meot, contracts the light, until it draws down the knowledge of emunah that everything in the physical world is connected above. "מַלְכוּתוֹ בַּכֹּל מָשָׁלָה."

## The Chidush

The halachic structure of testimony IS the structure of simcha. R' Natan placed them in the same halakha - Edut 4 - because they are the same thing:

1. **One witness can't stand in court** = one person alone can't stand against the force that keeps the light separated from the crumbs.
2. **You need two witnesses** = you need two to divide alafim into meot, to bring the light down to the crumbs, to include them back into the whole.
3. **That inclusion is the simcha** = "whoever doesn't know this secret doesn't know why he's happy" (Sichot HaRan 93).

HaTov veHaMeitiv maps onto the same structure from the halakhic side. The bracha for "He who is good and does good" can only be said when someone else is present and benefiting. The good literally requires a second person. Testimony, simcha, and bracha converge: getting the good takes two.

This is also why the etzot don't go into the seed as data. The encounter requires two. One witness - one who studies alone, or one who receives compressed advice - can't stand against the Sitra Achra. The etzot emerge as fruit of encounter, and encounter requires two.
