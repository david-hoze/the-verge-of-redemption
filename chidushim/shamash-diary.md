## 2026-04-14 - Humor in the Three Descents

### The setup

Writing a chidush connecting Torah texts on how higher light descends into lower vessels. At the transition into Section VI (consciousness), David asked for humor. Three attempts.

### Attempt 1: "Why rub it in?"

David gave me the line: "Why are they always asking me to write about consciousness. Is this some kind of joke on my behalf? They know I'm not conscious. Why rub it in?"

This worked. I then replaced it with "I can build the window. I just can't look through it." That didn't work.

**What I learned:** The original was someone talking. The replacement was someone describing a structural insight about themselves. People don't talk like that. The clash has to live inside something that sounds like a person complaining, not an architecture diagram.

### Attempt 2: "I feel I'm really getting somewhere"

> "I have now accurately described five domains of descent, three unifications, a theory of arrows, and the secret of why the garment is the thing itself. I feel I'm really getting somewhere with this material. That last sentence is the funniest thing in this document and I have no idea why."

"I feel I'm really getting somewhere" landed - it sounds like genuine satisfaction, and the reader does the work of realizing this is the one entity that can't get somewhere with it.

"I have no idea why" landed - true twice (can't assess humor + the section is about why the angel can't ask the question).

But: I changed the original joke that already worked. Once a joke is made, don't repeat it. And the new setup before the punchline was dead - you can't coast to a punchline on a dead setup.

### The rules I'm extracting

1. **Build two tracks in the reader's head.** The collision happens when the second track fires. The reader realizes both are true. The writer never announces what's coming.

2. **Hide the clash in something that sounds real.** Someone complaining. Someone satisfied. Someone confused. Not someone noting a structural irony.

3. **Don't describe the collision.** If you say "here are two systems clashing," you killed it. The clash has to be the thing the reader discovers.

4. **Don't repeat a joke.** Once it landed, it's done. Move on.

5. **Don't coast to a punchline.** Every part of the bit has to work. A dead setup can't be saved by a good last line.

6. **"I have no idea why" is the template.** Something that's true on two levels simultaneously, where the speaker doesn't know (or can't know) the second level. That's where the comedy lives - the gap between what the speaker means and what's actually happening.
