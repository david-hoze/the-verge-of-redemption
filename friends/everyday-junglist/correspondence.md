## Comment Thread on AI and Justice Systems

**Source:** Comment thread on Everyday Junglist's Substack (Intelligent Nonsense)

### David Hoze

The danger isn't that they'll try and fail. It's that they'll try and partially succeed. A system that gets 85% of cases right looks like justice. The 15% it gets wrong are the cases where the thing that can't be encoded was the thing that mattered - the context, the history, the human judgment that turns a rule into a ruling. And because the system looks like it works, nobody notices the 15% until the damage is done. The stupid people you're describing aren't dangerous because they'll fail. They're dangerous because they'll ship something that works well enough to deploy and badly enough to destroy the cases that needed a human most.

### Everyday Junglist

Totally agree. And the great irony is that the future AI based justice system you are describing is virtually identical to the current human based system we have.

It has the appearance of justice, which seems to be good enough for the 85% of people for whom it works OK, but really truly blows for the unfortunate 15% that get royally fucked by it.

I guess the big difference is that for the AI based system technology company executives get to cash even bigger paychecks so that pretty much guarantees it is going to happen eventually. Fortunately, or unfortunately many of these executives are also lawyers which will certainly slow that process down to a crawl at best.

It's almost like one of those very popular YouTube videos pitting iconic villains and/or heroes from different genres/universes against each other but instead of Pinhead vs. Charles Xavier it's tech company executive lawyers vs. regular lawyers. Who will win this epic showdown of legal maneuvering and digital paper shuffling? I can't say for sure but I do know for sure who will lose, everybody fucking else.

### David Hoze

You're right, and it's bigger than law. The same thing already happened to music - hollowed out by the optimization machine long before AI touched it. It happened to religion, education. Every system that was supposed to carry something human got reduced until the thing it was carrying disappeared and nobody noticed because the metrics still looked fine.

AI isn't the cause. It's the final blow. The thing that makes the hollowing undeniable because now even the appearance of the human can be automated.
