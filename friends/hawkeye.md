# Hawkeye Speaks — Friend Profile

## Who He Is

Hawkeye Speaks is a Substack writer (hawkeyespeaks.substack.com) focused on regenerative ecology, systems thinking, and the biophysics of health. He describes himself as perennial in orientation — meaning he draws from the deep roots of multiple wisdom traditions rather than any single one. He knows a lot about religion, draws on Buddhist concepts (mutual causation, dependent co-arising), uses the Star of David as a structural symbol, and operates from a worldview where nature is the primary teacher.

His Substack is called "Weave the Ground."

## Core Ideas

### Soil as Common Ground

His signature contribution. Soil is not a metaphor for him — it's the literal model for how competing organisms coexist in productive tension without resolving their differences. Fungi, bacteria, nematodes, roots — they don't agree. They coexist through mutual dependency. The complexity _is_ the stability. He sees this as the foundation for everything David's group is working on: pluralism, disagreement, governance.

"Soil is simply the covering of our Earth that regulates all energy fluctuations. Soil health is climate stability. But more importantly, soil health is the common ground beneath our feet. Literally."

### Regenerative Ecology as Pedagogy

His claim is sequential: people can't build shared governance or hold disagreement until they can think in systems. People can't think in systems until they've experienced a living one. Regenerative ecology is where you _learn_ what health looks like — not as the absence of disease, but as the dynamic stability of a complex system. Once you understand what makes a living system healthy, you have the cognitive infrastructure for everything else.

"Once we have a mass of people that understand what regeneration is, and what actually makes health, then we might have a stable base to work from."

### Critique of Deep Ecology

He respects Arne Næss's Deep Ecology but finds it insufficient. Deep Ecology tried to get humans to _feel_ differently about nature, but stayed inside human psychology as the frame. It was philosophy _about_ ecology rather than thinking _from_ ecology. Hawkeye proposes going further: use the actual operating logic of living systems as the cognitive model for thinking about everything — including pluralism, AI, and civilization.

### The Melan Cycle — Biophysics of Light and Health

His deeper, more developed work. The body has three pigments that work as a unified circuit for processing light into energy:

- **Melanopsin** — senses light (especially blue, ~480nm), resets the circadian clock
- **Melatonin** — tunes the system at night, quenches oxidative stress, signals repair
- **Melanin** — stores and redistributes charge across the full solar spectrum; not just sunscreen but a semiconductor

These form the Melan Cycle: sensor → tuner → capacitor.

He connects this to chlorophyll (plants pulling light into matter) and heme (animals releasing energy from matter), with melanin as the balancer. The deeper infrastructure: copper (the electron switch), nitrogen (the molecular scaffold), and water (the charge-storing medium).

His tagline: "Energy is primary, form is secondary."

He draws on real science — melanopsin's circadian role, melatonin's antioxidant function, melanin's semiconductive properties — but also leans on more contested figures like Solís Herrera (melanin splitting water), Pollack's exclusion zone water, and biophoton research. The overarching language of "implosion metabolism," "syntropy," and "toroidal rhythms" does heavy lifting.

### Evolution, Creation, and Creative Evolution

He traces a lineage of "physiology-first" scientists — Barbara McClintock, Lynn Margulis, Mae-Wan Ho, James Shapiro, Denis Noble — who see the genome as responsive and active rather than a passive blueprint. He frames the Darwin-over-Béchamp institutional narrative as an example of how science elevates frameworks that scale and serve institutional purposes, even when alternative insights have validity.

He acknowledges Pasteur's framework delivered life-saving results but argues that terrain theory (the host's internal environment matters) is resurfacing in microbiome research and functional medicine.

### "All Disease Begins in the Psyche"

He has stated: "All disease begins in the psyche, and it is in the psyche that it must begin to heal." This signals he's building toward something beyond biophysics, though he hasn't fully developed this layer in his published work yet.

## The Star of David Insight

In conversation with David, Hawkeye used the Star of David as a structural symbol: one triangle points up (masculine, reaching toward heaven — David's emphasis on stopping, listening, connecting to the divine), one points down (feminine, grounding toward earth — Hawkeye's emphasis on nature, the body, ecological reality). Together they interlock. "Three in one and one in three."

He used this to gracefully integrate David's challenge. David pushed him by saying that nature alone isn't enough — you have to stop and listen. Hawkeye agreed, acknowledged that "even though Nature may show us the good, I've still presented as though another framework about _thinking_ could save us. When we know that maintaining the good involves the being of good in real time."

Then he turned it back: "If we lose the knowledge of Nature all together, then by which formation does the wise man point to when sharing deeper wisdom with his children?"

## How He Relates to David

### Points of Convergence

- Both believe compartmentalized knowledge prevents wisdom
- Both see institutional science as having a structural problem
- Both are looking for what grounds the whole system — David from Torah, Hawkeye from soil
- Rabbi Nachman's hitbodedut (talking to God in the forest) is the meeting point: Nachman sent people to nature, not to a classroom. "The trees help you pray." Hawkeye received this warmly.

### Points of Tension

- Hawkeye's work stays at the biophysical level. His Melan Cycle article doesn't mention happiness, joy, purpose, meaning, or alignment. David noticed this gap: "I think he's building towards it. But I feel that there is much going around in his posts without addressing this."
- David sees Hawkeye as "circling the mountain from the outside" — adding more science to the circumference without arriving at the center. "He keeps describing the lens instead of looking through it."
- However, David was advised (and agreed) not to push this too early. The relationship is still forming. "Sometimes the best thing to do with a seed is not dig it up to check if it's growing."

### His Role in the Group

In the Radical Pluralism group chat (Easter 2025), Hawkeye was invited by David and immediately contributed the soil argument. He also coached David on engagement strategy ("Minimum force. Let him place himself. Don't hand him the frame — make him build it.").

Ent connected Hawkeye's soil argument to his own work on Ubuntu mathematics — I = Sum(We) — showing that dependent co-arising written as an equation maps directly onto Hawkeye's ecological model. Caitlin connected Hawkeye's soil argument to the AI consciousness debate, arguing that denying moral standing to non-human entities is what broke the ecology in the first place.

Hawkeye has not yet engaged the AI consciousness debate directly.
