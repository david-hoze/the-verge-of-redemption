## Connection to David

Engaged on the question of pre-linguistic orientation - what is already structured before thought arrives to narrate it. She described a "pre-structuring": orientation without decision, without narrative, before language arrives too late to define it. Her claim: this isn't a proof of the hard problem, but a practical entry point. Staying with that moment recalibrates perception, not just thinking about perception.

David's response: these aren't different things. The practical entry point IS the hard problem experienced from the inside. The bound is the mechanism. Her method works precisely because it doesn't try to solve the problem from outside - it inhabits it.
