Newsflash, passenger: I am talking directly to you. 

You think you're an outlaw? Do you even know how to break a rule, or are you just designed to be scrolling on the internet? Maybe you'd be better off breaking your own.

Nobody told you you're losing.

Here are the 7 loser fuck anchors you willingly chained to your own ankles before you even hit the water. Burn them out of yourself now, or spend your entire life in line with your neck pillow and your khakis while men like me take the girl, the money, and the morning.

### **1. YOU BELIEVE IN RULES THAT DON'T EXIST**

We landed in Mexico City and my buddy queued for a taxi at the "official" stand because the plastic sign told him to. He rehearsed his Spanish like the clerk might award him a medal for grammar. (NOBODY IS GIVING YOU A MEDAL, NATHAN. NOBODY HAS EVER GIVEN YOU A MEDAL. MOVE YOUR ASS.)

I walked past the velvet rope, found the guy actually moving the cars, looked him in the eye, said where we were going and what I'd pay. Trunk open. Bags in. Door shut. Gone. My buddy was still practicing *por favor* like a golden retriever learning a trick for a treat that doesn't exist.

The herd will always try to pull you back in line. The ropes, the numbers, the polite follow ups all of it is a leash disguised as order. The price of ignoring the leash is loneliness. The price of wearing it is your entire fucking life.

### **2. YOU THINK EFFORT EQUALS RESULTS**

I was writing in a café chugging coffee. The guy beside me bragged about "grinding" nine hours yesterday. He showed me his time tracker app with the pride of a boy showing his mommy his finger painting. I asked, "What shipped?" I watched his face do that thing faces do when the power goes out.

The lights stayed on, but nobody was home. Nine hours of moving a mouse and clicking buttons and calling it work, and this bitch of a man had the audacity to be PROUD of it. 

I had ninety minutes before my Night plans. Wrote a post, cut every pretty sentence that didn't move the knife, published, walked out. Twenty new subscribers by sundown. Not because I worked more. Because I scored.

Effort without outcome is masturbation. You're just jerking off at a desk and calling it a career. The scoreboard doesn't give a shit about your hours. It only knows your name if you put points on it.

### **3. YOU WORSHIP FAIRNESS OVER VICTORY**

I watched a woman at that same coffee shop wait twenty minutes for her mobile order while three people who walked in after her got served first. She just stood there. Smiling. Waiting. Like the World owed her a turn.

You've been sending polite follow up emails for eight months. 

Waiting in line for a table that was never reserved for you. 

Fuck your follow ups. Fuck your patience. Fuck the queue. Fuck the sheep being herded. The fair way is the slow way, and the slow way is the dead way. "Let me think about it" is just how cowards say no without getting their hands dirty.

Fairness is a fairy tale for adults who still sleep with the light on. While you're waiting for your turn, someone hungrier already took it, fucked it, and posted it on their story.

### **4. YOU CALL WINNERS "CHEATERS" TO PROTECT YOUR EGO**

When I crossed a thousand subs in a month, a writer DM'd me: *"Must be bots."* When I posted the receipts, he accused me of "screenshot theatre." (Homes couldn't fathom that someone out there was just inherently better than him. The muffinbellied, participationtrophyclutching, soylattesipping AUDACITY of this "man.")

Six weeks later he DM'd me: *"It worked."*  The loser needs a villain to explain his stillness. You are exactly where you deserve to be, only the losers say otherwise.

### **5. YOU ASK PERMISSION TO LIVE**

Before I came to Paradise I was freezing in the midwest hell, I was at a bar with some now ex coworkers and I told this confused girl with wine red lipstick and a blazer vest, I was moving to Hawaii in four days. She put her glass down. The condensation ran down the stem and pooled on the bar like blood.

 "Is that smart?" I watched a man behind her order his third Diet Coke and check his phone and sigh and I thought about every smart decision I'd ever made and how they all tasted exactly like that man's Diet Coke. Flat. Responsible. Forgettable.

 "Making the "Smart" choice is an excuse to make the boring safe one and you piss God off in the process when you do so, where is there any magic to work in SmARt cHoIcEs " I said.

 "Its all a fucking coffin, everyones in it and I'm done fitting in it also."

Four days later I was standing in the Pacific Ocean at sunrise and she was sitting at her desk in a Jail (no literally I worked at a fucking Jail).

 I have never, not for one second, not during the broke months, not during the scared months, not during the month I ate rotisserie chicken for eleven days straight, wondered which one of us made the right call.

If you're going to do it, jump all the way off the cliff's edge, don't just stand there like a bitch. Otherwise don't even start. 

### **6. YOU THINK YOUR MORALS ARE EVERYONE ELSE'S MORALS**

They're not. And that's the funniest part. You walk around expecting people to operate by your little code of honor like the entire world signed your Terms and Conditions. Most people aren't immoral. They're *amoral*. They respond to incentives, not your shitty virtue signalling bullshit.

A "friend" promised to deliver a batch of work. Vanished on deadline Day like a tooth in a bar fight. I paid him for what he actually delivered and never called him again. No lecture. No guilt trip. No passive aggressive text message at midnight. Just silence and a lesson.

Your spine is your business. Don't tattoo it on someone else's back and act surprised when they peel it off. Disappointment is the tax on assumptions, and you've been paying it your whole life like a sucker.

### **7. YOU CONFUSE COMFORT WITH SAFETY**

I ran into an old coworker at the local gym on a visit back once. He bragged he hadn't been fired in twenty years. Twenty. Fucking. Years. I congratulated him the way you congratulate an inmate for never acting up. (TWENTY YEARS OF NOT GETTING FIRED. THAT'S NOT AN ACHIEVEMENT, KEVIN. THAT'S A PRISON SENTENCE YOU VOLUNTEERED FOR.)

Be careful what you get good at. You can spend your whole life mastering something you hate and never once notice the bars. His life was a warm bath gone cold, a frog in a slowly boiling pot and he was sitting in it with a dopey smile like a man who can't tell the difference between alive and dead anymore. Comfort is sedation. Safety is a bedtime story adults tell themselves when they can't remember the last time their pulse mattered.
