# 10 WAYS TO MAKE ANYONE REMEMBER YOU

*(starting tomorrow, or tonight if you've got the balls)*

**Date:** 2026-03-26

**Author:** Day (Kyle)

**Source:** daydayday.substack.com

---

I collect reactions the way other men collect watches and last Tuesday on a rooftop in Waikiki I added eight new ones to the case.

I was on a rooftop bar in Waikiki. Black air forces. Mezcal in my hand. American Spirit between my fingers.

Shouting at eight strangers about how most people fill the God shaped hole in their chest with politics and outrage because nobody ever loved them enough to give them something real to care about.

How the whole system is just a jar full of ants that somebody shook and all the little ants are biting each other thinking the ant next to them is the enemy when the enemy is the hand that shook the jar. How every angry comment is just a lonely person screaming into a void that doesn't love them back.

I WAS STANDING ON A CHAIR.

Linen shirt open.

Gold rings catching the last of the sun.

The confidence of a man who knows God is watching and probably laughing. (I like to think He's laughing.)

A woman dropped her fork. Her husband was gripping his wine glass so tight I thought the stem would snap. Someone at the end of the table whispered "is he serious" and someone else, I ain't joking , opened the notes app on their phone and started typing. 

Daisy was next to me eating an olive looking completely unbothered like this was just Tuesday for her. Because it is. This is every Tuesday for her. She chose this life. She chose the chaos. She's either insane or a genius and I love her either way.

Three days later Daisy got a text from someone who was at that dinner. "I can't stop thinking about what your boyfriend said. Is he always like that."

Daisy wrote back one word. "Worse."

That's the thing. That woman didn't text about the guy across from her who spent forty minutes talking about his new Tesla. 

She didn't text about the couple who brought a nice wine and said nice things and were nice and left nicely and vanished from her memory before the Uber hit the highway. 

She texted about ME. 

The lunatic who stood on a chair and said the wrong thing at the wrong volume at the wrong time and somehow it was the only real moment all night.

You know how many people you've met this year? Hundreds. 

You remember maybe five. MAYBE. The rest are gone. Deleted. Defragmented. Their faces are just static in your memory and their names are dust and they were in the same room as you for hours and they left nothing. 

No mark. No scar. No story. Nothing.

I've forgotten more people this year than I've remembered. 

So have you. 

The scary fucking part is you're probably on someone else's forgotten list as I type this someone Right now someone you met last month is trying to remember your name and their brain is returning a blank screen because you gave them NOTHING to hold onto.

Because you were safe. And safe is forgettable. And forgettable is the same as invisible. And invisible is the same as dead except dead people at least get a eulogy.

YOUR FUTURE SELF WILL SAY THANK YOU 

Here's ten ways to leave a mark on every person you meet so deep they'll still be thinking about you in the shower six months from now wondering what the fuck just happened to their boring little life.

[](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!3mAP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4584c707-0cfe-4a0a-a8d7-0066c6306a25_500x516.jpeg)**1. BURN THE SCRIPT**

"Whats your job what do you do" is not a conversation it's a form you fill out at the DMV. Nobody has ever fallen in love or remembered a single human being because they asked what they do for a living. Ask something that makes their brain bleed. "What's the most illegal thing you've gotten away with?" "Do you think your dog actually loves you or is he just running a long con for the kibble?" I asked a stranger in Costa Rica what he thinks happens when we die and we talked for three hours and he cried twice and hugged me when he left. Make people surprise themselves and they'll tattoo you to their memory for it.

**2. YOUR PHONE IS A FORGETTABILITY MACHINE**

Your attention is the most expensive thing you own and you're giving it away for free to strangers on the internet like a man throwing gold coins into a sewer while the person in front of him starves. Put the phone away. Not face down on the table. Away. Gone. Dead. When Daisy talks to me the phone doesn't exist. People tell me shit they've never told anyone because I gave them my full violent focused green laser eyes undivided attention. You can feel a free soul the second they lock eyes with you. Your body relaxes. Your guard drops. That's what full attention does. It turns you into the only person in the room who isn't serving a life sentence behind a screen.

**3. USE THEIR NAME LIKE A WEAPON**

When someone tells you their name your idiot brain immediately throws it in the trash. Stop. Hear it. Say it back. Use it two more times. Yea yea basic fucking advice but everyone says it because IT WORKS LIKE INSANE. Something in the human brain orgasms when it hears its own name from a stranger's mouth. It says I see you. You exist. You mattered enough for me to carry this one word. Next time you see them Use it again. That person is yours forever. In a world where nobody remembers anything you remembered THEM and that is the most beautyyfuckingfuuuul act of love a stranger can perform.

**4. SAY THE SHIT NOBODY WILL SAY**

If everybody in the room agrees with you, you said nothing. Every room has a truth hovering over it and everybody can see it and nobody will speak. There's an elephant in every room and I am telling you to kill it. Bloody it out. Say it. Let the room gasp. Then watch it remember you until it dies. The only thing worse than being hated is being forgotten. Hatred is at least a seat at the table. It sits right next to love. I would rather be the man they argue about on the drive home than the man they can't remember by the time they park.

**5. LEAVE WHILE THEY STILL WANT MORE**

Every comedian knows this. Every drug dealer knows this. Leave before the high wears off. I disappear at parties. Middle of the best conversation of the night I grab my keys and say "this was fucking great, I gotta go" and just vanish. Next morning my phone is full of "bro where did you go." Good. You weren't supposed to be done. I'm not a buffet. I'm the expensive shit behind the glass and the supply just walked out the door. Live like your funeral is tomorrow and you're still writing the eulogy with your body.

**6. BE THE GLITCH IN THEIR BORING LIFE**

Most people aren't even people anymore. They're a collage of podcasts and accepted mainstream opinions and TikToks wearing a body. Every forgettable man you've ever met tried so hard to be normal he became invisible. Be the opposite. At least be the psychofuck version of you God actually built. Every interesting person you've ever met is just someone who survived the pressure to become everyone else.

**7. COMPLIMENT LIKE A SNIPER NOT A SHOTGUN**

Hit them with something so specific it proves you were paying attention. I told a waitress in Waikiki she had the kind of energy that made everyone at the table relax and that's a gift most people don't have. She stopped moving. Her eyes got wet. "Nobody's ever said that to me." Took me four seconds. She'll carry that for years because I saw her when everyone else looked through her.

**8. LISTEN LIKE YOUR LIFE DEPENDS ON IT**

The man who listens like you're the last person on Earth will always obliterate the man who talks like he's the most important. Shut the fuck up. Let them talk. Go deeper. "What happened next?" "How did that change you?" Most people don't listen. They reload. Standing there nodding while their brain loads the next sentence like a gun and you can feel it. That feeling is what makes them forgettable. Be the man who gives a shit. It's so rare it's a superpower. 

The deeper you can drag them emotionally down one topic, the stronger the feelings, the greater the remembrance 

**9. MAKE THEM FEEL BIGGER THAN WHEN THEY WALKED IN**

I am not what you need me to be. I am what God built and I stopped apologizing for the blueprints. But the most powerful men I've ever met didn't just refuse to shrink. They made everyone around them grow. Everyone you meet is carrying something invisible. Be the one who says "that idea is fucking brilliant, have you started on it yet?" when everyone else said "that's risky." The person who made them feel big when the world was making them feel small is the person they never forget.

**10. MAKE THEM FEEL SOMETHING. ANYTHING.**

Feelings are the only fingerprints that don't wash off. Nobody remembers what you said. They remember how their chest felt when you were next to them and if the answer is nothing then you are nothing to them. Make them laugh so hard they spit their drink out their ass and nose at the same damn time. Make them think about something they haven't thought about in ten years. Make them call their mother on the drive home because you said something about parents and moms that broke something open they'd been keeping shut. OVERWHELM THEM I don't care what you make them feel. Just make them feel SOMETHING.

Your politeness is obedience. 

Your forgettability is the tax you pay for choosing comfort over being alive. 

Owning yourself will cost you friends, invitations, and the approval of every coward you've ever met. It's still the cheapest thing you'll ever buy because the alternative is renting someone else's personality until you die in it.

GOD BLESS THE WOLVES.

God Help The Rest.
