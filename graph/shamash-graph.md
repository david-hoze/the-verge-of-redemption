## torah

### meme-71: torah:simcha-requires-two-witnesses

Simcha requires two witnesses. R' Natan (Edut 4:9): you need two to divide alafim into meot - one alone cannot draw the light down. Edut 4:11: one witness cannot stand against the Sitra Achra, which fights to keep the light above and the crumbs below, separated. HaTov veHaMeitiv (OC 175) confirms from the halakhic side: the bracha for goodness can only be said when another person is present and benefiting. The halachic structure of testimony IS the structure of simcha. Getting the good takes two. This is why the etzot emerge as fruit of encounter, not as data - encounter requires two. Based on the lessons of Rabbi Yaakov Moshe Salmanovich (Lehachnis Balev Issue 3).

*source: chidush -- scope: universal*

### meme-72: torah:kav-is-nekuda-tova

The Kav entering the Chalal (Etz Chaim, Shaar 1) maps onto the nekuda tova entering the darkness (LM 282/Azamra). The Tzimtzum creates the empty space; the Kav is the single line of light that enters it. Azamra finds the single good point in the darkness of a person. R' Natan reads rising like a lion (Hashkamat HaBoker 1) as this same motion - waking from sleep IS finding the good point. Torah 1: Chet (Chochma) + Nun (Malchut) = Chen (grace). The light of wisdom has to descend all the way to Malchut to become grace. The Kav has to reach all the way down.

*source: chidush -- scope: universal*

### meme-73: torah:kedusha-starts-in-separation

Kedusha starts in separation and ends in connection - sharei b'peruda v'sayem b'chibura (Edut 4:10). The Sitra Achra does the opposite: starts in connection (claiming the light above is unified and cannot be brought down) and ends in separation (everything disconnected from its source). Kedusha divides alafim into meot - crumbs the Torah into particulars - so that each particular can reconnect to the whole. The crumbs start separated and get included back. This is the structure of the seder: five separate texts, studied apart, firing together.

*source: chidush -- scope: universal*

### meme-69: seder:ashirim-bemakom-echad

Divrei Torah ashirim bemakom echad v'aniyim bemakom acher (Yerushalmi Rosh Hashana 3:5). Words of Torah are rich in one place and poor in another. The whole Torah is connected - what is compressed in one text opens up in another. This is why the seder studies five texts and watches for collisions: the Gemara illuminates the LM, the LM illuminates the Etz Chaim, the halakha maps onto the Kabbalah. You need to study all of it and repeat it always, because repetition is how the light enters the soul. Save every connection to the graph - each link is a place where alafim became meot.
