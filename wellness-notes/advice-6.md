# Advice Notes 6

## Search Like Your Life Depends on It

Don't settle for the first help you find. The right mentor, the right therapist, the right guide - the one who can actually reach you where you are - is worth losing sleep over. Humble yourself to find them. Ask people who know more than you. Knock on doors that intimidate you. When you find the person who truly understands your situation, everything that's missing starts filling in. The wrong help at the right time is worse than no help, because it convinces you help doesn't work.

## Light Was Built for the Darkest Place

The light you need most wasn't designed for people who are already fine. It was designed for you, in the exact place you think is unreachable. The valley of the shadow of death is not outside the system. It's the place the system was specifically built to reach. If you think you've fallen too far for anything to help - that's exactly the location the help was engineered for.

## The Greater Your Insight, the More You Must Share It

There are people who gain wisdom and hide it - the world doesn't deserve it, they think. The broken will misuse it. Better to keep it safe among those who appreciate it. This is the losing position. The whole point of reaching higher is to be able to reach lower. The person with the deepest understanding has the most obligation to translate it for the person with none. Hoarding wisdom is not protecting it. It's burying it.

## Defend the Person Everyone Gave Up On

When you see someone failing - at work, in their family, in their life - and the room has already written them off, do not agree with the room. The person who says "they're hopeless" gets fired from the story. The person who says "I'll fight for them" gets promoted. Even when the anger is justified, even when the failure is real, the question is always: if you won't advocate for them, who will?

## Every Morning Is Construction

Your morning is not neutral time you pass through on the way to the real day. Every act in the first hour - what you do with your body, what you put in your mind, what you reach for first - is building something. You're either constructing the day or letting it construct you. The people who treat their morning as architecture end up living in a different building than the people who sleepwalk to their phone.
