# Advice Notes

## The Weight Is Real But Incomplete

When you open your eyes and the weight hits - the failures, the distance, the "how did I get here" - do not argue with it. It is real. But it is not the whole picture. The mistake is thinking you have two options: accept the weight as the truth, or deny it with positive thinking. Both are wrong. The weight is real and incomplete. Those two things are not a contradiction.

## Search

The prescription is not "think positive." It is not "believe in yourself." It is: search. Find one thing you did that was good. Actively. The way you'd search a building after a fire. You're not hoping something survived. You're looking. How is it possible that you never did any good in your life?

## The Good You Find Will Be Contaminated

The time you helped someone but did it for the wrong reason. The project you started but didn't finish. The love you gave badly. It counts. The good point always shows up looking like failure. That's not a defect. That's what dawn looks like before it's bright.

## Your Worst Moment Has Building Materials in It

Your worst moment is not disqualified from building something. Your worst moment may be where the building materials come from. Not the moment itself - but the good that survived inside it.

## Include the Part That Stinks

Most self-help says: focus on your strengths. Ignore your weaknesses. Build on what works. The opposite is true. The weakness belongs in the recipe. Not because you celebrate it - but because excluding it makes the whole thing incomplete. A life that only counts the clean wins is not an honest life.

## Say It Out Loud

The good point needs to become speech. Tell someone. Write it down. Say it to God, or to the ceiling, or to yourself in the mirror. It doesn't matter who hears it. What matters is that you hear it. The transition from silence to speech is the transition from sleep to waking.

## Get Up

The heart lifts the feet. You don't wait until you're ready. The getting up is what ready looks like.
