# Advice Notes 7

## When You Can't Go to the Help, the Help Comes to You

When you're strong, you have to travel to what you need - seek it out, earn access, make the effort. But when you're truly broken, when you can't move, something shifts. The help comes to your door. Your inability to get up is what triggers the house call. If you're too wrecked to seek out healing, that doesn't mean healing can't find you. The system has a protocol for people who can't move. It's called mercy.

## The Person Who Needs You Is Your Gift

The friend who calls in crisis. The colleague who can't stop dumping on you. The family member who always needs something. You think they're draining you. The structure says otherwise: the person who shows up needing help is how you receive what you need. You can't get to the light directly. It comes through the act of giving. The needy person at your door isn't an interruption to your life. They're the delivery mechanism for something you can't get any other way.

## Looking Foolish Is the Price of Reaching People

The person who actually connects with the lost, the broken, the ones everyone else gave up on - that person looks ridiculous to the serious people. They eat with the wrong crowd. They joke when they should be solemn. They lower themselves in ways that make the respectable uncomfortable. This isn't failure of character. It's the cost of the mission. You cannot reach someone by staying above them. You reach them by meeting them where they are, and meeting them where they are will always look undignified to someone watching from higher ground.

## The Purest People Will Oppose the One Who Works in the Mess

The opposition to real outreach doesn't come from bad people. It comes from good ones. The most sincere, the most dedicated, the most genuinely holy - they're the ones who can't stomach watching someone engage with the broken. Their purity makes the mess intolerable. They feel righteous in their criticism, and they are righteous. They're just wrong. This has happened in every generation. The one who reaches lowest is always attacked by the one who stands highest.

## One Color Doesn't Fit Everyone

Reaching different people requires different forms. The insight that transforms one person bounces off another. The language that opens one heart closes another. If you only have one way of saying things, you can only reach one kind of person. The person with the most range - who can be formal and crude, simple and complex, funny and dead serious - reaches the most people. Wisdom isn't one color. It's a coat of many colors, and every color is for a different person.
