# Advice Notes 5

## The Sicker You Are, the Better the Doctor You Need

When someone is barely struggling, any advice helps. But the person in the deepest hole needs the most skilled guide - not the least. A mild problem responds to a generic solution. A severe one requires someone who can reach all the way down to where you actually are. Don't settle for cheap help when you're in real trouble. The worse it is, the more you need the best.

## When Your Mind Is Losing, Stop Using Your Mind

Not every battle is won by thinking harder. When the intrusive thoughts are winning - the spiraling, the obsessive loops, the thoughts you can't stop - do not fight them with more thinking. That's reinforcing the siege. Withdraw the mind entirely. Rest. Let something deeper than intellect hold you. You can pick the mind back up in the morning when it's yours again.

## You Were Made for One Thing

All the complexity of your life - the jobs, the relationships, the logistics, the noise - exists for one purpose: to come to know what is real. Not to believe it. Not to be told it. To know it, the way you know your own name. Everything else is scaffolding. The scaffolding is necessary but it is not the building.

## Understanding That Hasn't Reached Your Body Hasn't Finished Arriving

Knowing something in your head is not the end. The insight needs to travel all the way down into how you walk, eat, sleep, and hold your shoulders. If your body doesn't know it yet, you don't fully know it yet. The mind gets it first, but the work isn't done until the body carries it too. That's why practice matters more than theory.

## Grieve What Should Exist but Doesn't

There is a kind of grief that has nothing to do with loss - it's grief for what should be here and isn't. The relationship that should work. The community that should exist. The version of yourself that should have arrived by now. Don't push past that grief. It's not self-pity. Feeling the absence of what should exist is the first act of building it. The people who build real things always start by mourning what's missing.

## The Scream You Can Almost Hear

When intelligence gets captured by trivial purposes - when a brilliant mind is spent on things that don't matter, when someone's deepest capacity is wasted on noise - there is a scream. It's the sound of something valuable trapped in a place too small for it. You've heard it in yourself. The restlessness that won't go away, the feeling that you're built for something your life isn't touching. That's not anxiety. That's your intelligence calling from inside its cage.
