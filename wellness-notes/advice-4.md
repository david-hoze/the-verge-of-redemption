# Advice Notes 4

## Sleep Is Sometimes the Right Answer

When the thing tormenting you genuinely has no answer - not "I haven't found the answer yet" but "there is no answer available to a human mind" - stop thinking. The right move is to put the mind down entirely. This isn't giving up. It's the difference between resting a broken leg and refusing to walk. Some darkness you don't fight with intellect. You survive it by trusting that morning comes.

## Know Which Half of the Night You're In

There are two kinds of dark hours. In the first, the questions have no answers and the right response is to stop trying. In the second, the answers exist but you can't see them yet - and the right response is to get up and work. Most of your suffering comes from mixing these up. Grinding on the unanswerable makes you crazy. Sleeping through the answerable makes you stuck. Learn to feel the difference. When the weight lifts even slightly, that's your signal to move.

## The Gap Between Knowing and Knowing

You already know the things that would change your life. You know them intellectually and nothing happens. The problem is never information. The problem is that knowing something with your mind and knowing it with your whole heart are two completely different states. One produces opinions. The other produces fire. Almost everything that matters happens in that gap.

## Don't Comfort Yourself Before the Moment That Matters

The instinct right before something important is to soften it - eat something, scroll something, take the edge off. Don't. The slight discomfort is doing something. It's opening your throat. Comfort right before the critical moment is a sedative that numbs exactly the part of you that needs to be awake. Go in a little hungry.

## Every Day Is Day One

You are not continuing yesterday. You are starting. The previous attempt may have started wrong - wrong motivation, wrong angle, wrong energy. That's fine. Start now. The instruction is not "pick up where you left off." The instruction is "begin as if you never began before." The power is always in the beginning, and every morning hands you a fresh one. Most people waste it by thinking they're in the middle of something.

## All Beginnings Are Hard and That's Where the Power Is

Starting is the hardest part of anything. That's not a bug. The difficulty of beginning is where the force lives. The middle of a project runs on momentum. The beginning runs on something deeper. That's why you keep going back to it - not because you failed, but because the beginning has energy the middle doesn't. When something has gone stale, don't push harder through the middle. Go back to the start.
