The photography parallel is real - every new medium gets told it isn't art. And every time, the medium wins.

But that debate was about the producer's struggle. Is the photographer an artist? Does pressing a shutter count as making something? Photography answered yes by proving the eye behind the lens still has to work.

The note was about a different axis. Not whether AI can make art - but whether anyone's left who has to work to receive it. The photography debate never touched the listener's side. Nobody argued that looking at a photograph was too easy. Spotify did something photography never did: it eliminated the encounter from the receiver's end. You don't have to sit with anything. You don't have to choose. The algorithm chooses, and you feel whatever you happen to feel.

So the real question isn't whether AI follows the photography pattern. It's whether it matters that a medium "wins" if there's nobody on the other side doing the work of meeting it.
