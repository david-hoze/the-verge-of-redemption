# I PUNCHED A GIRL IN THE FACE

**Subtitle:** A Love Story
**Author:** Day
**Date:** April 13, 2026
**Source:** https://daydayday.substack.com/p/i-punched-a-girl-in-the-face

---

Some guys buy their girl flowers.

Some guys write poems.

I cracked a bitch in the jaw while Bob Marley's son sang about peace. We're all romantic in different ways.

Let me back up.

Let me tell you about last night.

I reach into the freezer and pull out a Coors Light.

Blue Mountain shining on the can bringing my face to a grin.

Daisy is in the shower singing "I shoooot the sheriff" at the top of her lungs

"Dais you want a beer?"

"Is water wet?" she shouts back.

I smile.

I reach into the shower and hand my angel a cold one.

She takes it without missing a lyric.

I strip down and hop in with her because life is short and showers are better together and Ziggy Marley is playing somewhere tonight and we're gonna be there drunk and beautiful and ready for whatever God throws at us.

In T-minus three hours I will be punching a woman in the face.

But I don't know that yet.

Three beers later we look at the clock.

FUCK.

The concert starts in ten minutes. We're still wet. We're already drunk. We're perfect.

We throw on clothes. I'm in all white pants, white shoes, and a vintage Tommy Bahama dark green linen shirt with cigars printed all over it. I look like a drug dealer on vacation. Daisy is deadly in a tight brown top and all white pants. We look like we hopped out of an Ace Ventura movie. But in like a hot sexy way. Like if Ace Ventura fucked a Bond girl and they had twins.

Daisy stashes shooters and two cold beers in her purse and we fly out the door and race through Honolulu to hear Bob Marley's son sing his daddy's songs.

Island life babyyyyy.

The sun has melted into the night sky. City lights taking over where God's light left off. I sip and it starts to blur together and I think to myself how beautiful life is. How lucky I am. How Daisy's hand on my thigh feels.

We swing into Ala Moana parking lot. Daisy pulls out the shooters. New Amsterdam vodka. Cheap and effective.

Fuck I feel like I'm in high school, she mutters.

We delete the shooters. She sips another one (YES DAISY SIPS ON SHOOTERS she's fucking psycho and I love her) I nurse the last silver bullet and we watch the concert goers stumble in.

Never in my life have I seen so many dreadlocks in one place. Like Bob Marley clones. I'M IN THE RASTAFARI MATRIX.

It's always funny the crowds artists bring in. You can see the threads. The commonalities. Couples. Families. Hippie dread types. Even a few white dreadheads who definitely smell exactly how you think they smell. Did you know dreads form from literally just not washing your hair and letting it tangle clump together. . . . yup

We let the line finish because I refuse to wait in lines. Lines are for sheep. Wolves arrive when they arrive. We stroll in arm in arm like we own the place because we do. We own everywhere we go. Not legally. Spiritually.

I sigger saunter stroll my way up to the bouncer. He waves his magic metal detecting wand all over me.

"Yes I am radioactive," I tell him.

He doesn't smile.

Nobody fucking smiles anymore. Nobody laughs anymore. Everyone is so serious all the time. Walking around with their shoulders hunched and their eyes dead like life is a prison sentence instead of a fucking miracle. LIFE ISN'T SERIOUS. Life is a party and most people showed up to stand in the corner and complain about the music.

Not us.

Never us.

It's packed inside. Wall to wall bodies. Ziggy isn't on yet. Daisy and I cut through the crowd toward the bar. Miller Lite for me. Vodka soda for her. Thirty dollars. I leave a ten dollar tip because money is meant to flow and stingy people die alone. We meander into the crowd.

I feel like a shark swimming through a sea of Hawaiians who I'm pretty sure are convinced Bob Marley was Hawaiian. Like they claimed him or something. Every island thinks it owns reggae. It's cute.

We find a spot. There's a pillar to our left so we edge up behind these two women to get a view of the stage. One of them is the tallest woman I've seen in years. Gotta be 6'5. Skinny as fuck. Tatted up arms and neck. Face like she'd been scowling since birth and it just stuck that way. Local girls. Her friend is shorter but worse. Masculine. Bitchy BUtchy. Built like a fire hydrant with a chip on her shoulder. Cargo shorts and a faded tank top that said something about Hawaii on it with the Hawaiian flag upside down.

They've got that energy. That "we hate everyone especially people who are happier than us and especially the pretty white blonde couple next to us" energy. I can smell it on them like they shit their pants.

They keep looking back at us. Mean little glances. I ignore it.

Ziggy comes on stage. His dreads are longer than my entire body. The music starts and it's alive. It feels like we've time traveled back to watch Bob himself.

The natural mystic flowing through the air. Everyone swaying. Daisy pressed against me. Her hips moving. My hands on her waist. We're dancing and I'm thinking this is it. This is liiiiiifffffeee.

Then gravity commits a crime.

Daisy's drink slips from her hand. It hits the ground. Splashes up. Gets on the two Hawaiian dykes.

They spin around ENRAGED. Eyes wild. Like Daisy just pissed on their ancestors.

"I'M SO SORRY," Daisy shouts.

"IT'S NOT OKAY."

I step in. "Hey. Calm down. Where's the one love ladies? No need to fight. We're not playing basketball."

The 6'5 one stares down at me. "Really?" she scowls.

I laugh and let it go because I'm trying to enjoy my night with the woman I love.

They turn back around. We keep dancing.

Then they start splashing water from their drinks onto Daisy's face.

I watch it happen in slow motion. The shorter one dipping her fingers in her cup. Flicking water at my girl's face. Giggling to her friend like they're in middle school. Like this is funny. Like Daisy is something to be laughed at.

They hated us because we were beautiful.

They hated us because we were young and free and in love in all the ways they weren't. They hated Daisy's blonde hair and her smile and the way I looked at her like she was the only woman in the room. They hated that we rolled up looking like movie stars while they stood there looking like they'd given up on themselves years ago.

They hated the light coming off us because it reminded them of their own shit stain darkness.

Some people see happy couples and feel inspired. Some people see happy couples and feel enraged. The second type will die miserable and hell fucked up wondering why.

"STOP DOING THAT," Daisy shouts.

They keep laughing. Keep flicking water.

And something in Daisy snaps.

DAISY SHOVES THE SHORTER ONE ONTO THE GROUND.

SHE TOPPLES OVER. HITS THE FLOOR.

IT'S ON.

The crowd erupts. Everyone turns. Ziggy is on stage singing "Could You be Loved" which is fucking hilarious in retrospect

The tall one GRABS DAISY BY THE HAIR AND YANKS HER DOWN FLAT ON THE GROUND AND----

I PUNCHED THE GIRL RIGHT IN HER FACE.

I saw red.

Like my blood became my eyesight. I didn't think. Thinking is for people with time. My fist was already moving. Thousands of years of protective instinct compressed into one motion. My knuckles connected with her jaw and I felt the impact travel up my arm like electricity. Her head snapped sideways. Her grip on Daisy's hair released.

You touch her. You answer to me. Simple.

Daisy is scrapping with the shorter one on the ground. Hair pulling. Screaming. She's got the dyke's tank top twisted in her fist and she's swinging with the other hand. That's my girl. I catch a flurry of hits to my face from people I can't see. Phantoms in the crowd swinging at the commotion like NPCs in a video game that just aggro'd on the nearest moving target. Doesn't matter. I keep my feet. I keep swinging. Adrenaline is a beautiful drug.

A security guard comes from behind. Big Hawaiian kid. Young and Fit. Arms like he grew up throwing nets and pulling fish. He hooks his arms under my armpits and starts dragging me backward. He's screaming in my ear:

"YOU DON'T HIT A WOMAN."

"SHE ATTACKED MY GIRL," I'm yelling back. "SHE'S GETTING AMBUSHED BY THESE TWO. I WAS PROTECTING HER. GO BACK IN THERE AND MAKE SURE SHE'S SAFE."

He stops dragging. Looks at me. Something passes between us. He nods once. Then he charges back into the crowd to find Daisy.

I slip away. Duck into the bathroom. Close a stall door. Call Daisy. She answers first ring.

"I'm in the crowd up front. I escaped. Meet me by bathroom baby."

That's my girl. My fucking girl. Scrapping with bitches twice her size and still smart enough to disappear into the chaos. It's such a relieving feeling knowing you can depend on someone to hold their own. To be smart. To be strong. To have your back when the world turns ugly.

We really were wolves in another life. I'm sure of it. Running through forests together. Hunting in the moonlight. Protecting each other from things with teeth. Some connections transcend lifetimes. Some people find each other again and again and again across centuries. I found her in Hawaii this time around. Next life maybe it's Tokyo. Or ancient Rome. Doesn't matter. I'd recognize her anywhere. I'd fight for her in any century.

She's by the bathroom when I exit. Our eyes meet. Both of us breathing hard. Both of us grinning like maniacs.

We start laughing.

We can't stop laughing.

Most couples will never know this feeling. Most couples are built for brunch and Netflix and polite disagreements about where to eat dinner. They exist in the safe middle where nothing beautiful ever grows because beauty requires friction and friction requires two people willing to bleed next to each other. Daisy and I are not most couples. We are the couple your mother warned you about. We are Bonnie and Clyde if Bonnie was English and Clyde had a surfboard and a Substack. We are the wolves that other wolves tell stories about.

We dip toward the bar. No line now because everyone's distracted by the commotion.

I order another round.

There really needs to be a barrier between me and the next drink because the absence of a line is basically handing me a loaded gun and saying shoot. Miller Lite. Vodka soda. We migrate to the left side of the venue. Fresh territory. New angle on the stage. Ziggy is still playing, still radiating that Marley DNA like a transmission from a cleaner dimension, and the music wraps around us like a warm current in cold water. We Watch from a distance as security drags the two dykes out of the building. The tall one is still running her mouth. The short one looks like she's about to cry.

The world always balances back out.

They started shit. They got aggressive. They put their hands on my girl. And now they're getting thrown out while we're ordering drinks and finding better spots with better views.

God has a sense of humor. He protects the wild ones and the lovers. I am both.

I grab Daisy. Pull her close. Kiss her feverishly in the middle of the crowd. Tongue and teeth and I don't give a fuck who's watching. Her hands on my face. Mine on her hips. The music swelling around us. Ziggy still singing about love. I think yeah. Yeah I know about that. I know about it more than you do, Ziggy. I just proved it with my fists.

We dance the rest of the night away. Swaying. Spinning. Laughing. Drinking. Making out between songs. My lip is split and I don't care. Her hair is wild from getting yanked and she looks like a goddess of war. We are victorious. We are invincible. We are the thing they make movies about. Most people spend their whole life trying to crawl out of the gutter. Not us. We live in the gutter and the stars at the same time. Beer in the shower and fistfights at love concerts and slow dancing in the wreckage and kissing in the chaos. We don't climb toward the light. We drag the stars down into the mud with us and make them shine there.

The concert ends. We stumble to the truck. Sticky Fingers comes on the radio. "How To Fly" playing as we pull out of the parking lot. Windows down. Warm Hawaiian air flooding in.

"Baby," she says. "You really cracked that bitch."

"She had it coming."

"Her face when I shoved her ovvvvveerr!!." Daisy starts laughing again. "She looked so surprised. Like she forgot consequences exist."

"Some lessons you gotta learn the hard way."

She puts her hand on my thigh. Squeezes. Looks at me with those blue eyes full of chaos and love and everything I've ever wanted.

"Us against the world," she says.

"Always."

We fall into bed still wearing our clothes. Fall asleep mid sentence. Tangled together. War torn and perfect.

I wake up and she's already looking at me.

Morning light through the curtains. Golden. Soft. Painting her blonde hair like a halo. She smells like vodka and sweat and that vanilla lotion she wears. She smells like last night. She smells like mine.

My head is pounding. Worth it. All of it worth it. A beautiful consequence. The world always balances out.

She kisses me before I can speak. Soft. Slow. Kissing the hangover away. Kissing the violence away. Kissing everything away until it's just us in this bed in this house on this island at the edge of the world.

"You punched that bitch so hard," she whispers.

"Nobody touches you."

"I know."

She knows. She's always known. From the first moment. She knew she was safe with me. She knew I'd walk through fire. She knew I'd throw hands with anyone, anywhere, any size, any gender, anyone who threatened what we have.

I'll punch a hundred women if a hundred women try to hurt Daisy. I'll fight the whole island. I'll fight the Devil himself. Anyone can get it. ANYONE!

We stay in bed until noon. Then we get up. Make coffee. Sit on the lanai. Watch the ocean sparkle. Her scalp is sore from getting her hair pulled. We retell it to each other a hundred times by now. Laughing just as hard each time. I watch her retelling it and my mind drifts off thinking how much I love this girl. If I loved her less I could probably describe it. But the feeling is so big it breaks the language every time I try. I can write about violence and life and the ocean and death but I cannot write a sentence large enough to hold what she does to me. The love is bigger than words and letters. It always has been.

This is what happens when you say yes. Yes to the shower beer. Yes to running late. Yes to the concert. Yes to standing your ground. Yes to protecting what's yours. Every adventure in my life started with a single yes. Every boring night started with a no.

Say yes. See what happens. Maybe blood. Probably the best night of your life. Probably both.

Ziggy was singing about one love.

He was right.

I've got one.

And I'll fight the whole fucking world for her.

Us against the world. Always.

Bonnie and Clyde with better fashion and worse impulse control.

Day and Daisy until God says otherwise.

GOD BLESS THE WOLVES.
