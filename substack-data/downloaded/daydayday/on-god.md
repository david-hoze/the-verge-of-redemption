# ON GOD

**Subtitle:** FERAL FAITH
**Author:** Day
**Date:** April 6, 2026
**Source:** https://daydayday.substack.com/p/on-god

---

It's 7 AM on Easter Sunday and the sun is climbing out of the Pacific like a body that refuses to stay buried.

How fitting I think to myself.

I am on the balcony in nothing but linen pants and a hangover. Miller Lite tallboy sweating in my right hand. American Spirit burning in my left. The smoke goes up to the thing I am praying to. The beer goes down to the thing I am praying about. This is the only liturgy I have ever been good at.

Puff. Puff. Puff. Sip. Sip. Sip.

I crush the can against my thigh and the aluminum gives like it knows what's coming. Through the screen door I can see Daisy stretching in our bed like a cat who has decided the sun is acceptable. Blonde hair everywhere. One leg out of the sheet. If God made anything more convincing than that, He kept it for Himself. And He gave her to me because He knew I had the teeth to keep her.

I have been scared to write about God. Scared like a man holding a loaded gun who knows what it can do in the wrong hands. But this morning I realized that fear was just the cleverfuckdevil whispering that I'm too filthy to speak the King's name.

The modern church makes me want to vomit.

Mega churches with pastel lighting and smoke machines and skinny jean pastors talking about all the feminized submissive sad fuck shit they spew. Songs sung in unison sounding like they want God to be their boyfriend. "LOVVEERRR OF MY SOUUUL" Rooms full of castrated men holding acoustic guitars, singing lullabies to a domesticated God, while their wives sit in the front row replacing their husbands with the pastor. Cuming with each word he says.

I refuse to participate in the gelding of the Almighty.

My faith is not a safety blanket. My faith is jet fuel. It is the cheat code that lets me live the way I do. People watch me run motorcycles at 120, surf the deep blue, write things that get me placed on lists, claim a gorgeous woman, live completely outside the norm, and they ask how I'm not afraid.

Because I'm a prodigal son who knows exactly who his Father is. I trust God's hand moving in my life. He didn't build me to sit in a cubicle and apologize for who I am.

The Bible is the most violent, beautiful, God breathed book ever written and pastors have spent two thousand years sanding it into a wellness pamphlet. David killed a giant with a rock, slaughtered a thousand men, cut their foreskins off as a bride price, then wrote love poems to God so beautiful they're still in the book three thousand years later. Samson pulled a temple down on three thousand enemies and died smiling. Jesus braided a whip with His own hands and beat the money changers out of His Father's house.

That is not a God who came to make you comfortable.

I flick the bike on and it fires up roaring like a lion hearing his name called. The ride to the beach church takes twelve minutes if you're a good whittle church boy. I do it in seven. Daisy's arms locked around my ribs, both of us laughing into the wind like the wind is something we just beat in a fight.

The pastor is Hawaiian. Arms like a fisherman. Voice like a man who has yelled at the ocean and won. No teleprompter. No acoustic guitar bullshit.

"He is risen."

I'm sitting in the sand with a beer in my hand and I feel something move in my chest I've spent two years calling indigestion.

He goes straight to the tomb. The women who showed up expecting a corpse. "Faith is not knowing," he says. "Faith is showing up anyway."

I think about the gun I put in my mouth at twenty. The violence I saw everyDay in jail. Every time God picked me up by the neck like a feral kitten and I rolled my eyes at Him. I've been calling it luck for two decades. It was grace.

"God doesn't call the qualified. He qualifies the called. He came for the ones who knew they were drowning. The qualified can stay home."

I've known I was drowning since I was nine years old.

After the service I wade into the ocean in my linen pants like a lunatic. Look up at the pink and gold sky and say it out loud without sarcasm for the first time in my life.

"Okay."

One word. The smallest white flag in history. A wave hits me at thigh level and almost takes me down and I laugh because of course He'd punctuate it. Of course He'd baptize me with a wave.

I am not your pastor. I'm a man who has lived like a wildfire and somehow not burned the forest down. The only explanation is Someone standing in front of the worst of it with His hands out, taking the heat that should have killed me.

If you've been running, I won't tell you to stop. But He is chasing you. And when you get tired, He'll be right there.

I'm going home to make breakfast with my girl. I'm going to keep smoking and drinking mezcal and being a feral mess in ninety ways because He knew what He was buying when He paid for me.

*Lord, keep my teeth sharp. Keep my heart open to You and closed to cowards who want to domesticate the life You gave me. Forgive my arrogance but use my violence. Thank You for the ocean. Thank You for the girl in my bed. I am a fire on this earth but Your son in the dark.*

*Let's ride.*

*Amen.*

**GOD BLESS THE WOLVES.**

He is risen. He is risen indeed.
