# THE SUN ALWAYS RISES

**Subtitle:** Dead goats. Brown water. Sharks. And the most beautiful sunrise I’ve ever seen. (Hawaii Flooded)
**Date:** APR 15, 2025
**Source:** https://daydayday.substack.com/p/the-sun-always-rises

---

God opened the sky up and washed Hawaii clean for the last two weeks.


Or dirty you could say. Dirtier than a stripper on laxatives fucked in the ass with all these mudslides.


The entire island flooded. Power gone for two days.


A house up the road knocked clean off its foundation, boulders the size of refrigerators sitting in the driveway like God rolled dice and the mountain lost.


Farmers ripping fences out in the dark because the alternative was drowning their livestock in the pens.


Homeless people’s dogs floating away in brown mud river streets. The dam threatening to break. The skydiving airport on North Shore completely underwater.


And goats floating upside down in the ocean.


Yea. Goats are negatively buoyant. They float upside down. Legs in the air like a biblical plague that didn’t make the final cut of Exodus because even God thought it was too weird. (I have a photo of the goats at bottom of the essay.)


The sharks came in for the feeding frenzy.


And on today’s menu: goats.


Then the sun came out at exactly 9 AM today. So


Ladies and gentlemen of whatever flood you think you’re drowning in.


The sun always rises. And it does not give a single fuck about your situation. Good.


Dance with her when the power goes out. Light the candles. Eat the cheese dip with your fingers. Take the shot of mezcal. Dance to the music until the laptop dies then dance in the dark because the dark is not the enemy. The dark is where you find out if you’re the man or just wearing a costume. Be their candle in the dark.


A woman who dances with you in a blackout will go to war with you in the Daylight.


Write the book by candlelight. I sat on my floor and wrote six pages of a novel while the rain tried to punch through the roof.


There is no better writing weather than the end of the world.


A man who only creates when conditions are perfect will never create anything worth a damn.


Drive through the flood. I drove my truck through streets with no stoplights. Brown water past the tires. Every light dead. Every road a guess. You learn what matters when the grid goes dark. Your nerve and your gas tank and whether the woman next to you screams or laughs.


Daisy laughed.


You will lose power. Literally and spiritually. The lights will go out in your apartment and in your chest on the same week and nobody will come to fix either one.


You will sit in the dark smelling like three day old sheets and you will believe this is it. That the version of you that had momentum and a woman who looked at you like you invented fire, that version is dead and floating upside down with the goats.


Good. Now you know what the bottom looks like. Most men never visit. They just fear it. You’re here. Look around. It’s not that bad. It smells like rain.


The flood comes for every man. The woman leaves and steals the smell of her shampoo on the pillow and the pillow smells like nothing and nothing smells like death.


The friend you called brother turns stranger.


You watch everything you built float away upside down in brown water.


That’s not the end. That’s the soil for it all to grow anew.


Every beautiful thing grows out of something that rotted first. The worst thing that ever happened to me built the best thing I ever made.


The Hawaiians have a saying: Uwe ka lani, ola ka honua. “When the sky weeps, the Earth lives.”


Two weeks of flooding on these islands and the soil will be blacker and richer than it’s been in years. Flooding or Farming? You can decide.


Notice everything. The goats. The sharks. The exact minute the light broke through. Most men sleepwalk through catastrophe and miracle with the same dead face.


They wouldn’t notice if the sky cracked open and angels started bartending. Be the man who is so viciously awake he can taste the difference between Friday and Saturday rain.


I’m standing on my balcony and I see him. Red spandex guy.


There he gooooes again.


Every neighborhood in Hawaii has one. Maybe 55, retired from something he never discusses, emerges from his garage in head to toe red spandex on a road bike every single Day.


Rain. Shine. Biblical flood. Clip in shoes. Never once acknowledged me in months.


Today he wheels the bike out, looks up at the sky like he’s checking if the sky is done pissing and crying all over Oahu, and starts stretching his quads on the driveway.


I lean over the railing and shout: “THANK GOD FOR THE DAY!”


One nod. Like a Roman emperor acknowledging a peasant who said something accidentally correct. Clips in. Rides through a puddle the size of a swimming pool without changing speed or expression.


Be the man that carries on. Yeaaa maybe Not the red spandex (definitely not). The principle. The man who has decided who he is and the entire Pacific Ocean can’t wash it off him. Not weather or a natural disaster. And not even the opinion of the shirtless screaming blonde on the balcony. Nothing.


Decide who you are. Then be that every morning regardless of the forecast.


Your storm is not special. Every man alive has watched his paradise flood.


The version of himself he was building drowned somewhere between Day four and Day nine. The flood doesn’t send a warning.


But the sun rose this morning over an island that was underwater yesterday. Over dead goats and shark fins and a trampoline floating in the ocean. Over a farmer counting which animals survived the night. It rose over all of it like none of it mattered because to the sun, it doesn’t.


The sun doesn’t check your record before it rises. It doesn’t care if you deserve it. It shows up for saints and shitbags with the exact same warmth.


The sun has been rising since before men existed to cry about the weather. It will rise the morning after the worst day of your life. It will rise the morning after you die.


My neighbor is having his yard cut. Leafblower screaming praise. The evacuations are over. A truck just flew by, bed full of German Shepherds barking crazy(i like to think they were singing with joy). The island is steaming. Rain evaporating off the roads like God pulled the whole place out of the oven. The sharks are still circling. The goats are still floating. And I am still here. Espresso in hand. Awake. Noticing. Alive.


Show up and watch the sun do what the sun always does.


Rise.


Despite you. Despite the flood. Despite every dead thing floating in the current.


Rise.


Rise so high that when Death finally shows up he has to sit down and catch his breath before he takes you.


And THANK GOD FOR THE DAY.


GOD BLESS THE WOLVES.


God Help The Rest.
