## The Hobbies That Make You Dangerous



Not all hobbies are created equal. Watching TV is not a hobby. Playing video games for six hours is not a hobby. Jerking off to increasingly specific categories of pornography is definitely not a fucking hobby.


I’m talking about shit that changes you. Shit that scars ya an makes you someone worth knowing.


Surfing


You will get held underwater until you’re certain you’re going to die. You will get your board slammed into your face. You will paddle until your arms feel like they’re going to detach from your body. And then one day you’ll ride a wave so perfect you’ll understand why people dedicate their entire lives to this lovefucking dangerous beautiful sport. You’ll walk back to your car with salt in your hair and blood from the reef on your feet and you won’t need anyone’s validation because the ocean already gave you everything.


Spearfishing


You hold your breath. You dive into the blue. You hunt something with a weapon you’re holding in your hand and if you fuck up it might kill you first. This is what your body was built for before civilization softened it into a desk chair. You come up with dinner and you cook it that night and you eat something you killed and you feel more alive than any restaurant could ever make you feel.


Motorcycles


One mistake and you’re dead or worse. Every ride is a negotiation with mortality. Your senses sharpen. Your reflexes quicken. You become dangerous in a way that women can feel from across the room. She looks at you and she knows you’re not afraid to die. That’s attractive as fuck. Because a man who’s not afraid to die is usually not afraid to live either.


Fighting


Boxing. Jiu-jitsu. Muay Thai. Whatever. Get in thereee with someone trying to hurt you and see what you’re made of. Get your ass kicked. Get up. Get better. Walk around knowing you can handle yourself if shit goes sideways. That knowledge changes how you carry yourself. Women notice. Men notice. Everyone fucking notices.


Lifting


Because weakness leaks into everything. Into your posture. Into your voice. Into the way you fold when you should fight. I get confident as FUCK every morning when I look into the mirror.


Cooking Steaks


Medium rare. Cast iron. Butter basted. Feed her something you made with your hands and watch her eyes change. Feeding someone is primal.


Reading in Public


Pull out a book at the bar. Read while people watch sports. Let them wonder what the fuck you’re doing. Let them see a man who doesn’t need a screen to be entertained. The girl three seats down is watching. She’s not going to approach. But she’s going to remember you. An undisturbed mind is the most dangerous weapon on earth.


Playing Pool


Badly at first. Then better. Then good enough to bet on. The game teaches you geometry and how to stay calm when money’s on the line. Also you look cool as fuck leaning over a table with a cue in your hand. Don’t argue with me on this. Girls yes you look hot doing this 10x.


Tipping Big


Walk into a restaurant like you own it. Order the ribeye rare. Drink the good whiskey. Leave 40%. The server remembers you. The bartender remembers you. You become someone who shows up places and makes them better.


Being Funny to Everyone


The barista. The Uber driver. The old man at the hardware store. Make them laugh. Make their Day less boring. Charm is a muscle and you build it by using it on people who can do nothing for you.


Drinking With Your Father


The whiskey after he tells you something he’s never told anyone and you realize he’s just a man who got old trying to keep you safe. Take that drink. Those conversations don’t come back.


Racing Something


Cars. Boats. Motorcycles. Feel what 120 does to your nervous system. Feel the fear. Override it. Cross the finish line and remember you’re more capable than you thought.


Writing


Sit alone in a room and bleed onto a page. Put words in an order no one has ever arranged before. Publish it. Watch strangers respond. Watch them say “this is exactly what I needed.” Thats you being created in God’s image expressing it just like he did when he wrote the BIBLE.


Fucking Your Girl Like It’s The Last Night On Earth


This is a hobby and a skill.. Make her forget her name. Make her forget every man before you. Make her grab the sheets so hard her knuckles go white and her back arches off the bed and she says things she’ll be embarrassed about tomorrow. Then do it again. And again. Until she’s ruined for anyone else.


## The Seduction of Having Somewhere Else To Be



You become attractive by having a life so fucking good that women become a bonus, not the main event.


When I’m thinking about the next swell, I’m not thinking about her. When I’m writing until 3 AM, I’m not checking my phone. When I’m on the motorcycle doing 90 down a coastal highway, she’s not even in my brain.


And she knows it. She can feel my attention pulling away toward something she cannot touch. And it makes her crazy. It makes her want to earn it back. It makes her work for something that used to be free.


This is not manipulation you fucking pussy goblin bitch. This is just having a fucking life.


## The Almosts



Twenty years from now the steak you didn’t order, the flight you didn’t book, the woman you didn’t kiss at the airport, the motorcycle you didn’t buy because someone said “be practical” those are the ghosts that haunt you. Not the mistakes. The almosts.


Take the drink. Not the sad drink at home alone on a Tuesday. The ALIVE drink. The mezcal at sunset with someone you love on an island you can’t afford. The whiskey after the wave that almost killed you.


Take the drink.


Get in the ocean. Let something bigger than your plans toss you around. The ocean doesn’t care about your five year plan. Good. Neither should you.


Fall in love with someone who scares you. Kiss her in the street. In the rain. In the airport. In front of people who are uncomfortable. Let them be uncomfortable. Their discomfort is a measure of their deadness, not your excess.


> “Do not go gentle into that quiet suburb. Rage against the beige walls. Rage against the khakis.”


## The Playing It Safe Trap



I have been broken, rejected, laughed out of rooms, dumped in three countries, fired from the only real job I ever had, and put a gun in my own mouth at twenty.


None of that killed me.


What almost killed me was the year I played it safe.


Safety is addictive. Comfort is its dealer. Both will shrink you into a man who fits inside a life he used to be afraid of and now calls home.


> “The purpose of life is to eat it raw. With your hands. Juice running down your chin. In a country where you don’t speak the language. Next to a woman who just told you something you’ll remember when you’re dying.”


My phone is at 3% now.


Daisy just walked up behind me and put her wet hair on my shoulder and asked what I’m writing.


“How to be unforgettable.”


“Am I in it?”


“You’re in everything.”


She smiles and I can feel her skin against mine and the moon is still up there watching and I gotta go because my battery is dying and she smells like salt and I’d rather kiss her than finish this sentence.


Build your life until it doesn’t need witnesses.


Master something until it masters you back.


Disappear into your craft until people feel your absence like a phantom limb.


Become so fucking interesting that validation becomes irrelevant.


You are going to die, and playing it safe won’t save you from that.


So get sun on your skin. Salt in your hair. Stories in your chest. Scars on your knuckles.


Live so loud that when you finally go, the silence is deafening.


> “Every Day a man should ride something fast, read something dangerous, cook something bloody, say something that makes a woman laugh, and do one thing that scares him enough to feel his own pulse. That’s the minimum. The floor. Not the ceiling.”


GOD BLESS THE WOLVES.
