## 01 THE WEAPON



How to sculpt a body so good someone literally has to say something


Here’s the protocol. It’s simple. It’s gonna suck. Do it anyway.


No breakfast. Your ancestors didn’t wake up and immediately shove food in their face. They woke up hungry and HUNTED. You’re gonna do the same. Wake up. Black coffee. Feel the hunger. Let it sharpen you.


Small lunch. I’m talking survival rations. A protein bar. A stick of beef jerky. A banana. A zero-cal soda if you need the taste of something. That’s it. This isn’t a meal this is maintenance. Just enough to keep you functional without triggering a bunch of insulin.


Dinner is where you eat. But here’s the rule: ONE INGREDIENT FOODS ONLY. Chicken breast. Steak. Potatoes. Rice. Eggs. If your great-grandmother wouldn’t recognize it as food don’t fucking eat it. No sauces with fifteen ingredients. No frozen garbage. Just real food that came from the earth or had a face.


Eat until you’re satisfied not stuffed. Push the plate away. You’re done until tomorrow.


One hour on the stairmaster. Every. Single. Day.


I don’t care if you’re tired. I don’t care if it’s boring. One hour. Stairmaster. Daily. No exceptions.


Read my articles. Put on a podcast. Put on music. Zone out. Let your legs move automatically. This burns roughly 700-1000 calories depending on your intensity. Tell me you won’t reach your goal burning an extra thousand calories every single day for eight weeks. The math doesn’t lie. The stairmaster doesn’t lie. Only you lie when you skip it.


Lift three times a week. Quick sessions. Maintenance only. You’re not building a bodybuilder physique you’re preserving muscle while stripping fat.


Bench press. Pull-ups. Push-ups. Some ab work so those fuckers pop when the fat melts off. That’s it. Thirty minutes. In and out. The stairmaster handles your legs. The lifting handles everything else. Simple.


You’re gonna cry. You’re gonna bitch. You’re gonna want to quit. The first two weeks are gonna feel impossible. Your body is withdrawing from food addiction like a junkie coming off heroin. Push through it.


By week three something shifts. You feel lighter. Sharper. The hunger becomes your friend. By week six you look in the mirror and see someone you barely recognize. ## THE SHRED MEAL PLAN



Exactly What I Eat Day by Day to Get to 10% Body Fat


This is not fun. This is EFFECTIVE. The fun comes when you’re standing at the pool looking like a Greek statue while everyone else is hiding in the shade. THE APOLLO DIET


THE PHILOSOPHY:


You’re not eating for pleasure. You’re eating for FUEL. You’re eating the minimum required to maintain muscle while your body burns its fat stores. That’s it.


THE EATING WINDOW:


You eat between 2 PM and 8 PM. That’s it. Six hours to eat. Eighteen hours to fast.


If you want faster results, shrink the window to 4 PM - 8 PM (20:4 fasting).


If you’re a savage, do OMAD one meal a day, just dinner.


Outside the window you consume ONLY:

- Water (lots of it)
- Black coffee (no cream, no sugar, nothing)
- Plain tea
- Salt/electrolytes if needed


Zero calories outside the window. Not “just a little snack.” Not “just some fruit.” NOTHING.


DAILY MEAL PLAN:


WAKE UP (6-8 AM)

- 16-32 oz water with salt or electrolytes
- Black coffee if needed
- Hunger is progress


MORNING / EARLY AFTERNOON (Until 2 PM)

- More water
- More black coffee if you need it
- You’re still fasting. The hunger is good. You’re burning fat right now.


11:30-2 PM BREAK THE FAST (Small Lunch)


Choose ONE of the following combinations:


OPTION A:

- 1 protein bar (Quest, RX Bar, or similar under 5g sugar)
- 1 stick beef jerky
- 1 banana


OPTION B:

- 1 can tuna or sardines
- 1 apple
- Handful of almonds (about 15)


OPTION C:

- 2-3 hard boiled eggs
- 1 banana
- Small handful of berries


OPTION D:

- Protein shake (whey + water only, no milk)
- 1 banana
- 1 string cheese


This is NOT a meal. This is maintenance. Just enough to keep you functioning. 300-400 calories max.


6-8 PM DINNER (Your real meal)


This is where you eat. One ingredient foods only. Build your plate like this:


PROTEIN (8-12 oz cooked weight):

- Ribeye steak
- NY strip steak
- Chicken thighs (skin on, more fat = more satiety)
- Chicken breast (leaner, less satisfying)
- Salmon filet
- Shrimp
- Ground beef (80/20)
- Pork chops
- Lamb chops
- Whole eggs (4-6)


CARB (1 serving):

- Baked potato or sweet potato
- White rice (1-2 cups cooked)
- Fruit (berries, apple, banana, mango)


VEGETABLE (Optional but recommended):

- Broccoli
- Asparagus
- Spinach
- Brussels sprouts
- Any green vegetable


FATS (Included in cooking):

- Cook in butter, ghee, or tallow
- Olive oil on salads
- Avocado if you want


SEASONING:

- Salt liberally (Celtic sea salt or pink Himalayan)
- Pepper
- Garlic
- Any herbs/spices without sugar


SAMPLE DINNER BUILDS:


THE STEAK NIGHT:

- 12 oz ribeye, seared in butter
- Baked potato with butter and salt
- Side of asparagus

- 2 large chicken thighs, skin on, roasted

Monday: Steak + potato Tuesday: Chicken + rice Wednesday: Salmon + sweet potato Thursday: Steak + rice Friday: Eggs + bacon (lighter day) Saturday: Steak + potato (reward for the week) Sunday: Chicken or fish + rice

Absolutely fucking NOT:

- Fried food unless you fry it yourself in good oil
- Fast food obvious

- Never drink outside your eating window
