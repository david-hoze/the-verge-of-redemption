Just read *She Remains* by Machine Poet. *Companion Part I* hit me hard with the raw shock of memory and absence. this one strikes differently — slower, quieter — and once again I’m impressed. tender in the small gestures, unsettling in the silence growing between Anna, a human, and Sol, a humanoid AI. what does it mean to love an AI? the comfort, the routines, the effort to pretend it’s normal, and the limits that always break through. there are no answers here, only the ache of living with the questions. 

i did find myself wishing, afterwards, we could have stayed even longer with the characters, to feel more of the time passing. but in the end it is human, fragile, melancholic. it stays with you long after the last page. bravo on this new piece, Machine Poet :)
