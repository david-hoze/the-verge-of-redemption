---**The Divine Optimization** is, from the very first lines, a satirical tale about a Church in freefall, trying to bring the faithful back with the latest tech — and algorithms. You read it thinking *“haha, yep, good one”…* but, as is often the case with Machine Poet, it’s much richer than that.

And then, little by little, the laugh catches in your throat: it turns chilling, with this growing sense of dehumanization.

It’s a fable, yes - but a fable that mirrors our world. You catch yourself thinking: *“Wait… we could actually end up there!”*

It’s deliberately over-the-top, sometimes almost cartoonish, and that’s exactly why it works: you see the drift instantly, like a spotlight in your face. You smile… and then you feel the absurdity.

Machine Poet tears apart the stupidity of tech worship in the service of profit at any cost -and, by contrast, brings out the beauty of humanity.
