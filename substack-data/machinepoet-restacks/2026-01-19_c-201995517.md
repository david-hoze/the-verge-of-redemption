---A different kind of gut punch from Machine Poet  told in reverse: you think you know where it’s heading, and yet you’re pulled into that creeping sense of inevitability… right up until it still catches you off guard. And when it hits, it’s not just a twist-it’s chilling. And that’s putting it mildly.

Because the machinery is cold long before the ending: everything turns into function, procedure, commodity, until people disappear behind their roles.

The style is dry, sharp, relentless - and it works precisely because it stays sensory and concrete, with zero ornament. I also loved the pacing: even in all that austerity, a few stubbornly human details slip through… which somehow makes the ending feel even more cynical.

Yes, some characters are heavily “typed,” but that serves the point: a world that dehumanizes through repetition. There’s one passage some readers will find divisive, but it fits the body-as-object theme, and at the very least, it doesn’t let you stay comfortable.

Overall: brutally effective. A simple, harsh reminder stay trapped in the rhythm of this system long enough, and you don’t just live in it… you become it. A machine.
