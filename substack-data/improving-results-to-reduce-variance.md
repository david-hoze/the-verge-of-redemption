## Songwriting Tutorial: How This Was Built

### 1. Start with what you already have

The first draft had the core: the God/good theology, the money verse, the chorus hook ("It's exactly what you buy"), and the wife/designed verse. Don't polish early. Get the bones down.

### 2. The chorus carries the argument

Three choruses, three escalations:
- "You doubt, you die" - the threat
- "Goodbye, goodbye" - the farewell
- "To live, or to die" - the real choice

Each chorus uses the same two lines ("It's exactly what you buy") but changes the landing. The repetition makes the shift hit harder.

### 3. Fill-ins come from pressure, not brainstorming

Don't generate options in the abstract. Write the lines around the gap, feel what's missing, and let the gap tell you what it needs. "Someone designed" came from the rhyme with wife/life. "Meds to get by" came from needing something sadder than "comply."

### 4. Less explicit, more down to earth

First instinct was intellectual: "your sin is a syndrome now," "naming what you're feeling and feeling's not the same." Too much thesis. The fix: "Your mom held your face / She didn't know why / She didn't know nothing / She just let you cry." Same point, zero argument. Just an image.

**Rule: if it sounds like an article, rewrite it until it sounds like a memory.**

### 5. Each verse comes from a different article

The song compresses the whole body of work:
- God/good/flood → theology (What God Actually Wants)
- Money/tracks → civilization critique (The Machine That Ate Everything)
- Progress/blind → anti-techno-optimism
- Wife/designed → substitute relationships (The Substitute for the Substitute)
- Kid/psycho-babble → the body count, psychology replacing tradition
- Mom verse → The Muscle You Stopped Using, presence
- Spreadsheet/meds → The Machine That Ate Everything, the grind
- Each other/together → The Network That Already Exists

### 6. The title is the opposite of the song

"Improving Results to Reduce Variance" - sterile corporate AI jargon. The song is raw and human. The title IS the thing the song is fighting against. The contrast is the statement.

### 7. Rap demands economy

Every line that worked got shorter through revision. "You don't have to know nothing" beats "You didn't need to understand." Rap doesn't explain. It asserts.

### 8. End with community, not the individual

First drafts ended with individual choice ("choose your own life"). The final verse flips to "we": "We just need each other / They taught us to fight / We can do it together / We don't have to be right." The song isn't about saving yourself. It's about coming back to each other.

### 9. Callbacks create structure

- "left behind" (verse 2) → "start from behind" (verse 7) - same word, opposite direction
- "someone designed" (verse 4) → "Your soul in the spreadsheet" (verse 6) - the designed world expanding
- "what you buy" runs through every chorus - the commercial transaction as metaphor for moral choice

### 10. Know when it's done

When the last verse says the thing the whole song was trying to say, and says it simply, it's done. "We don't have to be right" is the song's thesis - and it arrived last, not first.
