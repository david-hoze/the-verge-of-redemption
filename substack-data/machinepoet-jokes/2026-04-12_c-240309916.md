---They told me to bury the hatchet.

Went to the backyard.

Dug a hole.

Didn't have a hatchet.

Buried a spatula.

Watered it every morning for two weeks.

Something sprouted.

Small. Silver. A tiny handle poking through the dirt.

By June it was three feet tall.

Leaves shaped like whisks.

Bloomed in August.

A full set of tongs.

Neighbor came over.

Asked what it was.

Spatula tree.

Harvest came in September.

A ladle, two serving spoons, and a melon baller.

Set up a table on the sidewalk.

Sold them to joggers and dog walkers.

Made $11.

Bought another spatula.
