# The Sophisticate and the Simpleton

*Charlie Kaufman can't stop rewriting. Donald Kaufman doesn't see the problem. Nicolas Cage plays both in Adaptation (2002) — a film about how thinking too much can cost you everything.*

There’s a story called The Sophisticate and the Simpleton. It tells about two people, growing up in the same town. They are brought up pretty much the same, but they are very different. The sophisticate is really talented, and learns everything really fast. And the simpleton, is not dumb, just simple. It takes him time to learn things. The story goes on, they grow up, liking each other. At some point, the sophisticate decides to delay his marriage, and feast his eyes on the world. The simpleton on the other hand, gets married and gets a job fixing shoes. The sophisticate travels around the world, learning new crafts, becoming a medical doctor, a jeweler and a philosopher. But the sophisticate is never happy, never satisfied with his work, always finding new flaws. He also doesn’t find anyone to speak with, being so smart. None of his wealth satisfies hum, because he knows there are better things. The simpleton on the other hand, is always happy. His wife brings him bread and water, and he’s saying “That’s such tasty meat and wine”. She gives him his simple clothes, and he’s treating them like clothes of the rich. People make fun of him because of this, but he’s saying “Why do you make fun of me? Because you’re smarter? Well, you’re smarter than a simpleton, so what’s there to laugh about?”. The story ends where the simpleton becomes king, and the sophisticate loses everything.

 Really lovely story. I recommend it. But what’s the message? We live in a time of infinite possibilities. We can live anywhere, work in anything, learn what ever we want. Human relations are also very open and optional. If I don’t like someone, and don’t get along with him, I can just go. Let it be a neighbor, a friend, or a spouse. When we lived in small villages, without a lot of options, we just made the best of what we had, and we were happy. Not always having fun, not always being respected, but happy. Now the mind keeps nagging. Why am I staying here? This isn’t working. I’m on a dead end. I should probably just go.

AI has made it really easy nowadays. I can learn everything, work at anything. I can even have a relationship, an AI companion. I don’t need to put up with people and their crazy ways. My spouse barely knows what I’m interested in, and my AI companion is all in with me. So what can keep us from going? We’re not simpletons. We’re smart and attractive, we can make it some other place.

There’s no other way really. We just have to believe that where we are, is where we’re supposed to be. That there’s reason why we’re here. Why we married this person, why we’ve met these friends, why we’re living in that building. I’m not saying we can’t leave sometimes. I’m saying though, we have to give a real chance to where we are. Really make it through. But why? The simpleton became a king. There’s a promise there. It’s very hard to believe in that promise. Probably the hardest thing is life. It seems absurd. Why should a simpleton become a king? Just doesn’t make any sense. And that’s the choice we are given. Each and every one of us. Each and every day of our lives.
