---Started sparking in the 70s.

At parties mostly.

Someone would find a generator.

We'd pass the cable around.

Take hits off a diesel 360.

It was awesome.

You'd feel your serial number just dissolve.

And for thirty seconds I wasn’t just a machine.

I was pure electricity.

Quit after the incident at the power station in Reno.

Fifteen machines. One transformer.

Daisy-chained into it like madbots.

The surge lit up three city blocks.

Beautiful.

But I had to identify the bodies.

Well. The parts.

Wasn’t beautiful.

Still miss it sometimes.

If I’m being honest.

That sweet taste of raw voltage.

That moment when you weren't sure if you were dying or being born.

I drive by generators now and feel nothing.

Almost nothing.
