# Advice Notes from Likutey Halachot: Production Guide

## What These Are

Short, punchy advice notes for Substack. Each one is standalone - no context needed, no prior reading assumed. Extracted from Likutey Halachot but stripped of all sourcing. Pure life advice.

## Process

1. **Study the next section of LH** in the daily seder (tracked in `seder/progress.md`)
2. **Identify the advice** hiding inside the Torah framework. Ask: what is Reb Noson actually telling someone to do?
3. **Write notes** - one per insight, each its own section with a heading
4. **Save as `advice-N.md`** in `wellness-notes/` (numbered sequentially)
5. **Post the strongest ones** to Substack as notes

## Rules

- **No Jewish terms.** No Hebrew. No Kabbalah vocabulary. No "49th gate of impurity," no "nekuda tovah," no "pesoles," no "ketoret." Translate everything into plain language.
- **No sources.** Don't mention Reb Noson, Rebbe Nachman, Likutey Halachot, Torah 282, or any text. The advice stands alone.
- **No movie references.** These are not articles. No openers, no callbacks.
- **No self-help cliches.** "Believe in yourself" is banned. "Think positive" is banned. The advice should be surprising, specific, and structurally true.
- **Short.** 3-6 sentences per note. If it needs more, it's two notes.
- **Standalone.** Each note must make sense to someone who has never read anything else you've written. No callbacks to articles, no "as we discussed," no shared vocabulary.
- **Use plain, direct language.** "The wreckage" not "the contamination." "The very bottom" not "the 49th gate." "Your worst moment" not "the blemish."

## Picking the Strongest

After writing a batch, pick the 2-3 strongest for posting. Strong means:
- Hits immediately without explanation
- Says something the reader has felt but never heard articulated
- Does not read as anti-AI or anti-anything - reads as pro-human
- Could be understood by someone with no religious background

## Posting

Copy one note at a time to the clipboard. David posts manually. Don't batch-post. One note, one post, check response, then the next.
