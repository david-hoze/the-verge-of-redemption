# A Parent Who Erupts

Yelling doesn't communicate authority. It communicates the absence of authority. When you erupt, your child learns two things. First, nobody listens until someone is screaming, so there's no reason to respond to a normal voice. Second, the parent who screams is the parent who has lost control - which means there's nobody in charge. The child who "won" the argument by making the parent lose composure didn't win anything. They lost the one thing they need most: a parent who is calm enough to lean on.
