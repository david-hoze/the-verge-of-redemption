# The Second Time

The first time you do the thing, your body tells you it's wrong. Your chest tightens. Something flinches. The second time, the flinch is quieter. By the third time, it feels normal. By the tenth, it feels like a choice.

The boundary didn't move. Your ability to perceive the boundary moved. The danger was never the first time. It was the second time - the moment it stopped registering as a violation. The normalization is the mechanism. Everything after that is downstream.
