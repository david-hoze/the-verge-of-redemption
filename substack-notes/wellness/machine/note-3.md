# You Cannot Optimize Your Way to a Good Marriage

A good marriage requires the willingness to stay when leaving would be easier, to listen when you'd rather talk, to sacrifice something you want for something your partner needs. None of this produces anything measurable.

You cannot optimize your way to a good childhood. It requires a parent who is present - not productive, not efficient, but there. There in the boring afternoons when nothing is happening. You cannot optimize your way to community. Community requires waste - time "wasted" at the neighbor's table, attention "wasted" on people who will never advance your career. The things that hold human life together are orthogonal to productivity.
