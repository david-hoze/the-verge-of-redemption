# The Song Became a Product

The psalm was not entertainment. It was a technology for directing anguish toward meaning. The folk song transmitted navigational knowledge: how to mourn, how to mark passages, how to bury the dead. Then the machine got hold of it.

The psalm became the pop ballad. The folk song became the jingle. The epic became the franchise. Art stopped being something a community made together to navigate shared conditions and became something individuals consumed to feel something for three and a half minutes. Nobody will connect it to the music. Because the music will be beautiful.
