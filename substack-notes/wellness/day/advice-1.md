# Advice Notes - Day (Batch 1)

## Nobody Wants to Be Your Meal

A person who needs you repels you. A person who has built a life so full that you're a bonus - that's the person you can't stop thinking about. You can't fake this. You either have a body you've trained, a craft you're obsessed with, friends you'd fight for - or you don't. The frequency to broadcast is "I want you but I don't need you." And the only way to broadcast it is for it to be true.

## Silence Is the Final Exam

If you can sit in silence with someone you just met and neither of you reaches for a phone, you've already found something most people don't find for years. Silence is where the performance stops. Most people fail it because they're terrified of what happens when they stop talking. But the person who can hold silence with you isn't performing comfort. They're actually comfortable. That's rarer than anything they could say.

## Specific Beats Vague Every Time

"I had a great time" means nothing. "The way your nose scrunches when you laugh at your own jokes before you finish telling them" means everything. Specific is what proves you were actually paying attention. Vague is what you say when you were there but weren't present. This applies to compliments, apologies, and love. The details are the evidence that someone exists in your mind as a real person, not a category.

## The First Date Never Ends

Her presence is not her permanence. She is choosing you every day and she can choose differently tomorrow. The only thing preventing that is your refusal to become boring. People think commitment means the courtship is over. It's the opposite. Commitment means the courtship has no expiration date. The person who stops trying because they "already have" the other person is the person who loses them.

## Decisiveness Is Not About Being Right

Pick the place. Pick the time. Make the call. You can be wrong about the restaurant. You cannot be wrong about having a plan. A person standing in the doorway of their own life asking someone else which direction to walk is a person nobody follows. She can veto anything - but the starting position is that you showed up with intention. Asking too many questions isn't consideration. It's outsourcing your spine.

## Kill the Small Talk Fast

"What do you do for work" should be illegal. Ask what they wanted to be when they were eight. Ask what song they play when nobody's watching. Ask "why" three times until they've accidentally told you something they've never told anyone. The voltage between laughter and honesty is the most addictive frequency a person can emit. Make them spit out their drink, then make them go quiet with something so true it lands like a fist to the chest.
