# The Tradition Doesn't Command Joy

There are 613 commandments. They command action with extraordinary specificity. What time to pray. What to eat. How to dress. They almost never command feeling.

"You shall rejoice in your festival" is structured not as an internal command but as an external one. Make a feast. Invite the stranger, the orphan, the widow. The tradition commands the conditions. The joy is expected to arise from the conditions, not from the command. You cannot command the song. You can build the room where the song is likely to happen.
