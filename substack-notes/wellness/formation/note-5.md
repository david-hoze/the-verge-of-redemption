# The Song Nobody Heard

A woman loved a prayer. She wanted her children to love it too. So she commanded it. Stand up straight. Say the words clearly. Feel what I feel. Her children grew up and never said that prayer again.

If she had sung it - just sung it, every Saturday morning, without checking whether anyone was watching - her children would have heard something they couldn't resist. Not the words. The frequency. The sound of a person who has forgotten she is being observed, who is simply present inside the thing she loves. That sound is the one that transmits.
