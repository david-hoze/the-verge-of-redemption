# Sadness With a Rope

A person is lowered by rope into deep water to retrieve something precious from the bottom. As long as the rope holds - as long as the connection to someone above the surface hasn't snapped - the descent is not a drowning. It is a retrieval mission. Isolation predicted mortality even after controlling for loneliness. The feeling isn't what kills. The structural fact of having no one holding the rope is what kills. One person. One rope. Sadness with a rope is a dive. Sadness without one is an ocean.
