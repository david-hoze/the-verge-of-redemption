# The Mat That Won't Fit Through the Door

Your narrative about yourself is a woven mat. You've built it from resemblances and comparisons - things that happened mapped onto what they mean, filtered through every pattern from movies, books, and other people's stories. The narrative is large. It covers a lot of ground. And it won't fit through the door to the next version of you.

The solution isn't to fold it smaller. It's to break through the wall. Not by destroying the pattern-matching engine - you can't, and you shouldn't. By finding access to the checking function that doesn't depend on catching the pattern after it's formed.
