# The Letters Fly

A man was burned alive wrapped in a scroll. His students asked what he could see. He said: the parchment burns but the letters fly into the air.

Propositions are parchment. Bound to the paper, the study, the replication crisis. When the journal retracts, the proposition burns with it. Stories are letters. They detach and survive - in memory, in retelling, in the mouth of the grandson who heard it from his grandmother. The fox doesn't need a journal. The fox survives because you told someone about the fox and they told someone else.
