# The Fox Is a Citation

A fox starves himself for three days to squeeze through a hole in a fence. Gorges on grapes. Tries to leave. Can't fit. Starves himself again. Squeezes back through. Exactly as hungry as when he entered.

The psychological literature calls this hedonic adaptation. Brickman and Campbell coined the term in 1971. The fox has outlived every paper on the subject. Not because it's more precise. Because you can see the fox, feel the hunger, remember the hole. The proposition gave you something you can argue with. The fox gave you something you can't get rid of.
