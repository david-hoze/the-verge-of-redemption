# The Torch Is Not Self-Help

A blind man walks through the streets at night carrying a lit torch. Someone asks: why? You can't see. He says: as long as I have this torch, people see me and save me from the pits.

You won't see the pits. That's not a temporary condition that better self-awareness will fix. It's structural. But you can carry a torch - show up visibly, in community, in a practice, in a room where other people can see you. Their sight becomes your navigation. The torch is not self-help. The torch is the act of being findable.
