# The Ball of Gas

Some people catch fire for about an hour. They read a book, attend a retreat, hear a talk, and something ignites. They feel different. They feel like they've arrived. Two weeks later, they're back where they started. That's not a character flaw. That's a physics problem. The fire had nothing to burn. Excitement without structure is a ball of gas that enters the atmosphere, lights up, and vanishes. You don't need more ignition. You need fuel. And fuel is boring - it's daily practice, repetition, showing up when the fire is out. The flash was real. It just wasn't enough.
