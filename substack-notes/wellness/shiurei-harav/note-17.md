# They Danced

Fifty men were herded into a room to die. They started dancing. The guards, who expected weeping, were confused. They pulled them out: "You think this is a dance hall?" They were told they'd be killed in the morning. That night, an order came to transfer workers somewhere else. They were short exactly fifty. The fifty who danced were loaded onto trucks and driven to safety. The guards came in the morning to an empty room. Nothing in this story is a strategy. Nobody danced to survive. They danced because that's what they were. And what they were turned out to be what saved them.
