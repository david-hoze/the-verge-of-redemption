# He Refused to Dance

God waited 2,400 years for someone to sing. Adam didn't sing after being created. Abraham didn't sing after being pulled from a furnace. Isaac didn't sing after the knife was lifted from his throat. Jacob didn't sing after wrestling the angel. Then King David danced in the streets when the Ark entered Jerusalem. His wife told him he looked undignified. He said he'd become even more undignified if that's what it took. David earned the eternal dynasty - not because he was the smartest or the holiest, but because he was willing to look like a fool in public. Generations later, his descendant Chizkiyahu defeated the Assyrian army - the largest military force the ancient world had ever assembled - and the whole world was watching. He could have been the Messiah. That was the offer on the table. All he had to do was what his forefather David had done: sing. But Chizkiyahu was a scholar. A serious man. He didn't sing. He took the foreign dignitaries into the Temple and showed them the Ark and the Tablets instead - as if the victory were a technology, not a relationship. The opportunity closed. David's own descendant, carrying David's blood, couldn't do the one thing David had shown him how to do. The thing standing between you and what you're meant to become might not be a mountain. It might be a dance you're too proud to join.
