# The Man Who Fixed Windows

Everyone in the community was celebrating. Singing, dancing, the room alive with joy. One man - the most overlooked, most disrespected person there - was handed a ladder and a box of tiny glass panes and told to repair the broken windows while everyone else rejoiced. He climbed the ladder. He started gluing. And he cried one tear. That single tear, shed while doing the job nobody wanted, was the thing that qualified him. Decades later, he was the one who carried the torch. Not the singers. Not the dancers. The man on the ladder.
