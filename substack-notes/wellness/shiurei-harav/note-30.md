# You Thought You'd Arrived

The moment you feel like you've figured it out is the moment to worry. Certainty is the ceiling, not the floor. The people who know they don't know are the ones still capable of learning. The people who know they know have stopped. The dangerous thing about a plateau is that it doesn't feel like a plateau. It feels like the summit. You unpack your bags, build a house, and call it the top of the mountain. Meanwhile the actual peak is invisible from where you're standing - because you'd have to admit you haven't arrived to see it.
