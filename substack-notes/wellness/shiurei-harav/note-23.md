# The Seed from the Fire

A man jumped into a fire on a gamble. He waited to see if someone else survived first, then jumped. It wasn't pure courage. It was calculated risk. But the act itself - the body entering the fire, regardless of motive - produced something three generations later that pure courage couldn't have. His granddaughter became one of the strongest people who ever lived. Your imperfect act still counts. Your mixed-motive generosity still builds something. Purity of intention is overrated. The fire doesn't check why you jumped. It only checks that you jumped.
