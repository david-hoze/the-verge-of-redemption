# The Dirt Rises

When you get close to something real - a teacher, a practice, a person who sees you clearly - the first thing that happens is that everything ugly in you surfaces. Anger you didn't know you had. Jealousy. Pettiness. Pride. It feels like the approach is making you worse. It's not. It's a pot on the fire. The dirt was always there. The heat is just bringing it to the surface where you can see it. If getting close to something good makes you feel terrible about yourself, you're probably in the right place. The discomfort is the mechanism, not the malfunction.
