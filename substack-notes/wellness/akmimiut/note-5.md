# Washing Off the Night

At night, the spirit weakens. The limbs grow heavy. Sadness strengthens in the absence of movement. In the morning, the spirit renews. The first thing you do is wash the night off your hands.

Every single day, you accumulate darkness. Every single morning, you wash it off. The renewal is structural, not motivational. The machine erases everything every night. Every morning, on the train to Montauk, you meet it again.
