# The Restlessness Is Not the Disorder

The child looking out the classroom window - the one who needed someone to say "what are you looking at? Tell me. I want to see what you see" - got diagnosed instead.

Your restlessness is not a disorder. It is the remembering trying to happen. The dream you had as a kid - the ridiculous one you stopped telling people about because they smiled in that way that meant "that's cute, now let's talk about something realistic" - was not a child's fantasy. Children are closer to their mission than adults. They haven't yet learned to be embarrassed by it.
