# The Therapist Dropped the Direction

The therapist kept the empathic listening, the safe space, the fifty minutes of undivided attention. But the therapist dropped the direction. Not by accident - by training. Clinical neutrality forbids telling you what your life is for. That's a "value judgment."

CBT can teach you to reframe a thought. It cannot tell you why your life matters. The patient asks the one question that matters - "why should I keep going?" - and the therapist, trained in neutrality, offers coping strategies.
