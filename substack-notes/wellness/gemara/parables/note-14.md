# When You Win and Someone Dies

A man was publicly humiliated in front of everyone. The people who could have stopped it had good reasons not to. Every one of them was technically correct. The humiliated man went home and set the whole thing on fire. That's the rule: you can be right about the principle and still be responsible for the catastrophe. The question was never whether you had a point. The question was whether your point was worth more than the person standing in front of you.
