# The Wrong Celebration

When a ship leaves harbor, everyone cheers. When a ship arrives, nobody notices. It should be the other way around. The one leaving hasn't proven anything. The one arriving survived the journey. You celebrate beginnings - the new job, the new relationship, the new year, the fresh start. Nobody celebrates the person who finished. Who endured the middle. Who arrived battered and leaking but arrived. The departure is hope. The arrival is proof. And you keep throwing parties for hope while ignoring proof.
