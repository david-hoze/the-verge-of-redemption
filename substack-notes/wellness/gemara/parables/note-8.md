# The Cost of Being Right

A scholar found a bandit and turned him into his study partner. For years they argued, pushed each other, built something neither could build alone. Then one day, in the heat of an argument, the scholar threw the bandit's past in his face. The partner died of grief. The scholar went mad. He got what he wanted - he was right, unchallenged, alone. That's the price. Every time you win an argument by going for the throat, you kill the only person willing to fight with you. Being right and being alone are the same destination if you don't know when to stop.
