# The Moment He Stopped Asking

A man spent his whole life running from himself. When it finally caught up, he begged the mountains for help. Nothing. The stars. Nothing. The heavens. Nothing. Every external thing he'd ever leaned on refused him. So he put his head between his knees and wept - and that was the moment it worked. Not because crying is magic. Because he finally stopped outsourcing his crisis. Every institution, relationship, and substance you've used as a buffer has the same answer: this one's yours. The work starts when you stop asking someone else to do it.
