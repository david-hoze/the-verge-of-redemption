# It Wasn't Me

A king hired two guards for his orchard - one blind, one lame. He figured neither could steal alone. The lame one climbed on the blind one's shoulders and they cleaned the place out. At trial the blind one said: I can't see. The lame one said: I can't walk. The king put them together and judged them as one. That's what you're doing when you split yourself into parts to avoid responsibility. My body did it, not my mind. My emotions did it, not my judgment. My past did it, not my present. You're one person. The king knows.
