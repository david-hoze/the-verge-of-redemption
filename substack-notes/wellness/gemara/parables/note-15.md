# Have You Seen My Father

A child riding on his father's shoulders looks around and asks a stranger: "Have you seen my father?" He's sitting on him. That's you complaining that nobody supports you while standing on the foundation someone built for you. That's you asking where the help is while using the help. The ingratitude isn't malicious. It's structural. When someone carries you long enough, you forget you're being carried. You think the height is yours.
