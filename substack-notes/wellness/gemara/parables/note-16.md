# This Too

A man was sent to deliver jewels to a king. On the way, thieves stole the jewels and replaced them with dirt. He said: this too is for good. The dirt turned out to be exactly what the king needed to win a war. The thieves heard about it and brought their dirt to the king. It was just dirt. The difference wasn't the material. The difference was the posture. "This too is for good" isn't optimism. It's a refusal to let the story end at the worst chapter. The people who try to copy you without carrying what you carry just have dirt.
