# One Bandage at a Time

The person everyone is waiting to save them sits at the gate among the sick. Everyone else unwraps all their wounds at once. He unwraps one at a time - rebinds it before touching the next - because he might be needed any moment and refuses to be caught completely undone. That's the whole method. You don't heal everything at once. You work one wound, stay functional, and keep yourself available for what's coming.
