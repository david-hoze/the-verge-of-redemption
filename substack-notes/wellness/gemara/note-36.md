# Go Back to the Cave

A man spent years in isolation doing the deepest work of his life. When he came out, everything he looked at burned - his gaze was so intense it destroyed whatever it touched. He was told: go back, you're not ready.

The wellness industry would celebrate this man. The tradition sent him back. Intensity without gentleness doesn't illuminate - it incinerates. The hardest part of deep inner work isn't the work. It's coming back soft enough to live among people who haven't done it.
