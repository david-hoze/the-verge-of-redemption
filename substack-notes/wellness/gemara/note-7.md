# The Second Time

The first time you do the thing, you know it's wrong - your chest tightens, something in you flinches. The second time, the flinch is quieter. By the third, it feels normal. By the tenth, it feels like a choice.

The boundary didn't move. Your ability to perceive the boundary moved. The danger was never the first time. It was the second - when it stopped registering as a violation. The normalization is the mechanism. Everything after that is downstream.
