# The Mountain and the Hair

The person who fought their worst impulse and won looks back and sees a mountain. How did I get past something that big? The person who lost looks back and sees a single hair. How did I fail to get past something that small? Both weep. Both are wrong.

The fight was neither as large as the winners think nor as small as the losers think. That's hindsight bias - the most precise description of it ever written, in a text from the 3rd century. No psychology paper has matched the economy of that image.
