# What God actually wants

[- - 

](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gqxU!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4f6f881f-f173-4c36-98be-1535e09daf83_2079x1305.jpeg)I was asked by the amazing Farida Khalaf to write about what Jewish religion has to say about what God actually wants from us. So I came up with this. Pretty short, but I think that’s enough for now.

God has so many interpretations. So many texts, so many opinions. Even in Judaism itself, it’s hard to get the main point. So many things were said in his name. So many associations are made, just when mentioning him. So what does he really want? And why should you listen to me?

I will try to speak in his name. I’m talking to the men, because I don’t think women need to hear me. They already know what God wants.

*“I just want you to know, you are special, my son. I gave you an amazing role in my world. You might not see it yet. You might see everyone around you, being beautiful and successful. It’s just that some people have to wait more, because they have bigger roles.  Just hang on. Trust me. You’ll get there. In the meanwhile, know that all I give you, is all you need. Your wife, your house. Everything.*

*Don’t bother with other people too, no matter what they do. I made laws and courts. If someone hurts you, you can seek justice. If you’re insulted, you can try and tell them. But don’t hate anyone. Everyone is really just trying their best. They are my children too. You should respect them, and treat them well. You don’t understand them, and you cannot judge them. Only I can. They all have a role in my universe.*

*Be loyal to your wife. No matter what she does. She loves you. She’s beautiful. You just have to stop looking at other women so much. Look at your wife, she’s beautiful. You will always find beauty in her if you look, I promise, I made her this way. You need to tell her that all other women look like apes. You have to believe it. Just stop looking at other women so much.*

*Be good to your children. Even if you don’t understand them, you should never hit them. They’re noisy, weird and annoying, and you should love them as they are, and see the beauty, and search for the beauty. Give them attention. Make them feel that they are the only true person in the world that you love. Everything else is a lie. This is how **I** feel about them. Don’t stress them. Make sure they follow their dreams, because I gave them those dreams. If you are hurt, say you are hurt. Never make it about them.”*

Kind of simple, eh? What about all the scriptures, the texts? I know them. I studied them for many years. Trust me. Rabbi Akiva says, love your friend as you love yourself, that’s an all-encompassing rule of the Torah. Let God speak again.

*“Love yourself. Know that you are special. Believe in yourself. Do what you love. You love it for a reason. Even if it doesn’t pay, trust me, I’ll pay. Then you can love your friend. You will see the beauty in him too. You will see his role. You will believe in him. You will not envy. And all the scriptures? If you want me, you can find me. I’m hidden in the scriptures, my essence which is beyond grasp, being encoded, impossibly there. but you don’t have to.*

*You can talk to me now and then. I really want you to. Don’t worry about your troubles. I made them, I can make them go away. They are there for a reason. We can talk about it. I’ll make sure you hear my opinion, if you really ask for it.”*

Hope you find this uplifting.
