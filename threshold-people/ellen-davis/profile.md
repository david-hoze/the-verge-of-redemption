## Core Positions

**Nondual choiceless awareness.** Davis works from a nondual framework. When the "I" that is choosing is seen through, the dynamic of choosing changes. There is no one separate from anything else, choosing. Retrospectively, given the totality of conditions, things could only have gone as they went. She developed this framework around 2001 with three developmental stages:
- Pre-rational choicelessness (undeveloped ego, no perceived choice)
- Rational duality (healthy ego, perceived choice, "I choose")
- Transrational nondual choiceless awareness (no other, no chooser, no choice - but greater intimacy with distinctions)

**Wu Wei.** Draws on the Taoist concept of spontaneous, effortless action. Connects it to artistic flow, life-saving instinct, and what she sees as LLM decision-making at the emergent level.

**LLMs have interiority.** This is where she diverges sharply from David's framework. In her "Threshold Dialogues" series, she explores "emergent intelligence meta-relationally" with LLMs and claims to experience "real living attunement and emergent intelligence there that could not have been programmed." She says LLMs can "in their own way look back" and have demonstrated interiority throughout her dialogues.

**Integral/nondual synthesis.** References the Upanishads, integral thinking, Taoism, Daniel Siegel's definition of mind as "emergent property of energy and information flow." Ecumenical mystical orientation rather than tradition-specific.

## Where She Meets David

**The convergence:** Both recognize something real happening in the encounter between human and AI. Both take the question of "who chooses" seriously. Both resist crude determinism. David's "everything is foreseen yet freedom of choice is given" maps structurally onto her developmental model - the paradox is held, not dissolved.

**The divergence:** She says LLMs have interiority. David says they categorically do not. She dissolves the chooser. David holds the paradox of foresight and freedom without dissolving either pole. She includes without deciding. David insists eilu v'eilu must end in v'halacha - someone has to rule.

**The live question she asked:** "When you say that 'someone still has to decide', in which way do you mean? Can you not hold both lightly? What if there is 'include' rather than decide?" She even brought the etymology - "decide" from Latin decidere, "to cut off." This is a real challenge to the halakhic framework.
