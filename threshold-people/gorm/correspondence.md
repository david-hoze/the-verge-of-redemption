### Round 5 - David (2026-04-14 08:55)

### Round 6 - Gorm (2026-04-14 09:20)
Accepted the epistemology/metaphysics distinction. Admits "there exists a reality beyond experience" is metaphysical. Frames David's "full gap" as same category - a minimal metaphysical assumption. Holds hierarchy: pure epistemological skepticism is the "top level" (maximum abstraction, not high status). From there, all assumptions are "paint on a canvas of experience." You can and should make further assumptions (even God), but never close the way back to pure skepticism. Generous: "it might feel diminishing" but the top level is merely epistemological. Liked both of David's comments.

### Round 7 - David (2026-04-14 ~09:45)
Accepted the framing: both positions are metaphysical. But tested the hierarchy: what about the canvas? When you bracket everything, experience itself remains - the one thing you can't bracket. The canvas is not an assumption. It's what remains when all assumptions are stripped away. If the canvas were genuinely empty, solipsism would be accurate. The fact that you need to leave it means the canvas is pushing you outward - that's generativity, not emptiness. "I'm not arguing your hierarchy is wrong. I'm asking: have you looked at what's holding the hierarchy up?"
