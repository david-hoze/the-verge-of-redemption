# Wild Pacific

## Who He Is

Neurodivergent, gay, Russian-origin tech professional living in California. Bilingual writer (English/Russian). Middle-aged. Writes under "Wild Pacific" (previously "Silent Ocean") on Substack - 59 subscribers, zero commerce, writes for himself. Tagline: "No commerce. No promotion. Acceptance. Travel."

- **Substack:** wildpacific.substack.com - 59 subscribers, ~50 posts since October 2024
- **Reads:** Sam Harris, Joscha Bach, Jon Haidt, David Shapiro, Buddhist Philosophy, FIRE
- **Format:** One-liners, short essays, poetry, fantasy fiction (D&D), sci-fi. Most posts 100-350 words.

---

## Core Themes

**Body over mind.** His deepest conviction. "Thoughts and logic are not better than emotions." Defends primal instinct, embodiment, "brainless sex," the wild pre-rational depths underneath rational consciousness. Cites Panksepp on layered consciousness.

**The second mountain.** Central allegory - outgrowing validated narratives, cultural heroes, comfortable frameworks. Past the first climb. Either ascending the second or standing at the base wondering whether to bother.

**Difficult interfaces.** Defends people who are socially awkward, autistic, aloof - not malicious, just hard to read. Pushes back against modern culture's tendency to discard anyone labeled "toxic" without distinguishing defensive awkwardness from genuine manipulation. Deeply personal.

**Organic vs. rigid systems.** Society needs both structure (conservative) and flexibility (liberal). Neither alone suffices. Liberals should stop performing righteousness and build.

**Anti-nostalgia.** Rejects received wisdom, crystallized knowledge, tradition-as-hoarding. "I'm not into traditional."

---

## The Contradiction

In his own writing: the body, the primal, the felt experience is what matters most. Embodiment is foundational. Intellect serves the animal, not the other way around.

In a chat with David: humans are just a stage. Technology is the next stratum of life. We'll fade away and machines will shoot for the greater spheres.

His Substack is the best argument against his chat position. He spent fifty posts celebrating what machines cannot have - and then told a stranger it doesn't matter.

## The Exchange

Met in a Substack chat (April 2026). Wild Pacific argued that machines are a logical next evolutionary step and humans will "watch from below and fade away." David engaged through several rounds - ecology, the tree metaphor, agency - before landing: "You're not describing a theory about machines. You're describing a theory about whether your life meant anything. And you've answered no."

## Assessment

Not a friend. Not a respondent. A stranger who said something that revealed more than he intended. His position isn't intellectual conviction - it's exhaustion wearing a philosophy. His own writing knows this. Whether he does is the question.

If he replies, the reply will tell you everything.
