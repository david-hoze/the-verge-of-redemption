The world screams. Notifications snap. Engines rumble. Conversations collide.

Everything moves fast. Too fast.

You scroll. You stop. You scroll again. Your thumb taps. Slides. Climbs back.

One screen replaces another. Your eyes sting. Your jaw clenches. Your breath blocks, releases, blocks again.

You switch apps. Return. Leave. As if truth might finally appear… in the next update. Your stomach twists with a vague emptiness. Your fingers tremble slightly. Your shoulders cave forward.

Everything is here. Everything demands. Nothing soothes.

Now imagine. You unplug. Just for a moment.

You close your eyes. The darkness behind your eyelids. One breath, just one. Then another. Your shoulders open. Your belly softens. Your fingers stop searching.

You are not in a rush. You have missed no train. There is nothing to chase. You find again your weight, your rhythm, your body.

Then your gaze opens to what is here: the branch bending, the passing of an animal, the distant voice of a stranger, the silence wrapping around.

Everything continues without you — and with you.

And there you see: this time was never empty. It is full. It is the source. The strength that prepares your movement.
