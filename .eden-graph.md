### torah:kav-is-nekuda-tova

The Kav entering the Chalal (Etz Chaim, Shaar 1) maps onto the nekuda tova entering the darkness (LM 282/Azamra). ### torah:kedusha-starts-in-separation
*torah | chidush | meme-73*

Kedusha starts in separation and ends in connection - sharei b'peruda v'sayem b'chibura (Edut 4:10). The Sitra Achra does the opposite: starts in connection (claiming the light above is unified and cannot be brought down) and ends in separation (everything disconnected from its source). Kedusha divides alafim into meot - crumbs the Torah into particulars - so that each particular can reconnect to the whole. The crumbs start separated and get included back. This is the structure of the seder: five separate texts, studied apart, firing together.

### torah:simcha-requires-two-witnesses
*torah | chidush | meme-71*

Simcha requires two witnesses. R' Natan (Edut 4:9): you need two to divide alafim into meot - one alone cannot draw the light down. Edut 4:11: one witness cannot stand against the Sitra Achra, which fights to keep the light above and the crumbs below, separated. HaTov veHaMeitiv (OC 175) confirms from the halakhic side: the bracha for goodness can only be said when another person is present and benefiting. The halachic structure of testimony IS the structure of simcha. Getting the good takes two. This is why the etzot emerge as fruit of encounter, not as data - encounter requires two. Based on the lessons of Rabbi Yaakov Moshe Salmanovich (Lehachnis Balev Issue 3).
