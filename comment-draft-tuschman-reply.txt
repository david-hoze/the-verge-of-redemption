Richard, that answer is exactly what I was hoping for. The sketchbook nobody sees. The guitar you play for yourself. Not as a break from the real work - as the thing that the real work can't touch.

I wrote something recently about exactly this. A bus driver in Paterson, New Jersey who writes poetry in a notebook and never publishes. The film is Jim Jarmusch's "Paterson." The argument is that the art is inseparable from the life that produces it - and that the moment you optimize it for an audience, something essential changes. Not the craft. The relationship between the maker and the thing made.

Your sketchbook is Paterson's notebook. The guitar is the same thing. The fact that you don't show them to anyone isn't a lack of ambition. It's the preservation of something that the professional world structurally cannot hold.

Here's the piece: https://davidhoze.substack.com/p/the-poem-the-bus-driver-wrote

I think you'll recognize it.
