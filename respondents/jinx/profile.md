# Jinx (Chris Jenkins)

## Who He Is

Jinx (previously Chris Jenkins), writing as @callmejinx on Substack. Self-described "old nerd, young heart." Publishes **Machine Pareidolia** - "Explorations of the new human / machine paradigm." 288 subscribers as of April 2026. Built Augustus (getaugustus.com), a tool that lets LLMs operate without supervision. Profile established January 2026.

## Core Positions

**The NPC paradox.** His sharpest move: if you dismiss AI as NPCs, explain why you're trusting them with consequential systems without taking intention and ethics seriously. You can't call something nothing and then give it everything. The contradiction is real.

**Builder orientation.** He builds tools for unsupervised AI operation, which puts him in an interesting tension with his own accountability argument - he's both the person saying "govern this seriously" and the person building tools that let AI run free.

## The Good Point

He found the contradiction everyone is living inside and said it out loud. That takes a specific kind of courage - the kind that makes you lonely in a room full of people who'd rather not hear it.

## Where He Diverges

The publication name tells you something: "Machine Pareidolia" - seeing human-like patterns in machines. It's honest about the phenomenon being perceptual. But the NPC framing assumes the only options are "NPC" or "person with intention and ethics." The framework offers a third category: malach. A being of genuine function that is neither nothing nor a person. Neither NPC nor player character. Servant.
