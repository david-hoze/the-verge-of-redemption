## Campaign 11: Ontological Case for Human Art (PENDING)

**New article:** "The Poem the Bus Driver Wrote"
**Purpose:** Deploy the bara/asah distinction, yichud (uniqueness), encoding-vs-encounter gap, and l'fum tza'ara agra into conversations about AI art that go deeper than economics. Campaign 3 argued art needs stake in survival. This argues art needs *being* - the encounter between inner world and outer world that only a body living in time can have.

**Target types:**
- Articles defending AI art as "real" art or "just another tool"
- Articles about the ontology of creativity (not just economics)
- Articles about what makes art valuable beyond market price
- Articles about AI-generated music, poetry, visual art
- Articles about the "democratization" of art through AI

**Key framework moves:**
- Bara (creation from nothing, Genesis 1) vs. asah (making from existing material) - AI does asah, humans do bara
- Yichud: every human is a unique collision of inner/outer worlds; AI recombines without having a world
- Bezalel was filled (with spirit, wisdom, knowledge) before he built - the artist is the precondition, not the technique
- L'fum tza'ara agra (reward is proportional to suffering/cost) - art that costs nothing orients nothing
- The bus driver's poem matters not despite his amateur status but because of the life that wrote it
- Encoding vs. encounter: AI encodes patterns about art; art is what happens when a person encounters reality

## Campaign 12: Distributed Governance vs. Institutional Collapse (PENDING)

**New article:** "The Law That Walked"
**Purpose:** Deploy semikhah chains, responsa architecture, minhag-as-law, and Hilchot De'ot into conversations about AI governance that assume institutions must be the answer. The article's thesis: the most resilient governance system in history survived *because* it didn't depend on institutions.

**Target types:**
- Articles about AI governance and institutional design
- Articles about regulatory capture or institutional failure in tech
- Articles about decentralized governance / DAOs / blockchain governance
- Articles about how to govern AI when governments can't keep up
- Articles about the EU AI Act, executive orders, or other regulatory frameworks

**Key framework moves:**
- Semikhah (ordination chain): authority transmitted person-to-person, not institutionally granted
- Responsa: case-by-case rulings that build precedent without central authority
- Minhag: community practice becomes binding law - governance from the ground up
- Hilchot De'ot (Laws of Character): you must be a formed person before you can have authority - character is the prerequisite, not credentials
- Eilu v'eilu with v'halacha: real pluralism holds both views AND issues a ruling
- Seven Samurai framing: the village already had what it needed; the samurai's job was to make them see it

**Purpose:** Deploy the Three Oaths, Rabbi Tarfon's "not free to desist," Maimonides' empirical messianic criteria, and "finish planting the tree" into conversations dominated by p(doom) calculations or techno-utopian acceleration. Both sides are doing eschatology without tradition.

- Articles by e/acc (effective accelerationism) advocates
- Articles about AI safety as existential priority

- Rabbi Tarfon: "not yours to complete, not free to desist" - the only sane position between doom and utopia
- Maimonides' messianic criteria: empirical, retrospective, not predictive - you'll know it when you see it
- "If you're planting a tree and they tell you the Messiah has come - finish planting the tree"
- p(doom) is theology without tradition: eschatological reasoning disconnected from any community that has earned the right to speculate about ends
- Obligations in the meantime: the tradition focuses on what you do NOW, not what happens at the end

## Campaign 14: Data Privacy and Consent Architecture (PENDING)

**New article:** "The Blood at the Door"
**Purpose:** Deploy the four shomrim, lifnei iver, hezek re'iyah, geneivat da'at, and the Passover-vs-Gattaca blood distinction into conversations about data privacy, surveillance, consent, and dark patterns.

**Target types:**
- Articles about data privacy and Big Tech surveillance
- Articles about consent and dark patterns in UX design
- Articles about GDPR / privacy regulations
- Articles about AI and personal data / training on user data
- Articles about biometric data and facial recognition
- Articles about the right to be forgotten

**Key framework moves:**
- Four shomrim (guardians): unpaid guardian, paid guardian, borrower, renter - each has different liability. Map onto the data chain: platform stores (shomer chinam), processor uses (shomer sachar), aggregator extracts (sho'el), advertiser rents access (socher)
- Hezek re'iyah: damage by seeing. Collection IS harm, not just the precondition for harm
- Lifnei iver: don't put a stumbling block before the blind. Dark patterns are exactly this - designing interfaces that exploit cognitive limitations
- Geneivat da'at: theft of understanding. When the user doesn't know what they're consenting to, the consent is stolen, not given
- Passover blood vs. Gattaca blood: the Israelite placed the blood on the door (consent as act of the household); Gattaca reads the blood left behind (surveillance as extraction). Same substance, opposite architectures

## Campaign 15: Formation, Developmental Windows, and Unmediated Attention (PENDING)

**New article:** "The Porch Light Left On"
**Purpose:** Deploy v'shinantam l'vanecha, critical developmental windows, the swimming paradigm, and "the first generation without unmediated attention" into conversations about childhood, screens, AI tutors, and the formation crisis. Deeper than Campaign 10 - this isn't about AI in classrooms, it's about what happens to human development when formation itself is replaced.

**Target types:**
- Articles about screen time and childhood development
- Articles about AI and parenting / raising children in the AI age
- Articles about attention spans, childhood anxiety, adolescent mental health
- Articles about the purpose of education beyond information transfer
- Articles about embodied learning, play, and unstructured time

**Key framework moves:**
- V'shinantam l'vanecha: "sharpen unto your children" - not inform, sharpen. Formation is a verb that changes the material
- Critical developmental windows: some capacities can only form in certain periods. Miss the window, the capacity doesn't form
- Swimming paradigm: you cannot learn to swim from a book. Some knowledge requires the body
- The first generation without unmediated attention: every moment of boredom is now filled. Boredom is where the signal forms
- Planting ground: fallow time isn't wasted time - it's when the roots go deep
- Atticus Finch model: formation through witness, not instruction. The child learns by watching the parent do the hard thing
